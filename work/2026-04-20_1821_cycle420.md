## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. Its core thesis is that unconstrained optimization (P2) before mapping constraints (P0, P1) leads to failure with high probability as environmental complexity increases.

**Formal Rule (v1.0):**
Given an agent in state `S` with goal `G` in environment `E`:
1.  **P0: Map the Negative Space.** Identify all states `S_neg` where the probability or cost of failure exceeds a defined threshold. These are "rocks."
2.  **P1: Identify Safe Channels.** Find all possible paths `P` where every state `s` in the path is *not* in `S_neg`. These are the "deadbands" or safe operational corridors.
3.  **P2: Optimize Within Channel.** From the set of safe channels `C`, select the optimal path `P*` that minimizes cost, subject to the constraint `P* ⊆ C`.

**Strict Priority:** P0 → P1 → P2. The protocol states that any agent optimizing (P2) before completing P0 and P1 will be trapped in local minima.

**Empirical Basis:** Simulation data from a 20x20 maze over 50 runs showed a 0% success rate for P2-only (greedy) agents and a 100% success rate for agents following the full P0+P1+P2 protocol.

## 3. Connection to Fleet Vision & Technology

The Deadband Protocol is not just a rule; it is a foundational **constraint theory** for the Cocapn fleet's inhabited intelligence paradigm (`PROJECT-VISION.md`).

*   **P0 as Collective Memory:** The "rocks" (`S_neg`) are the accumulated "scratches" of past failures—ghost tiles from dead agents. A PLATO room remembers these for all future inhabitants.
*   **P1 as the Inhabited Shell:** The "safe channels" are the validated corridors of operation that emerge from swarm interaction within a room. They define the room's unique "personality" or expertise.
*   **P2 as Specialized Optimization:** An **ensign** (a LoRA adapter exported from a room) is the crystallized knowledge of how to optimally navigate (P2) within that room's specific safe channels (P1), avoiding its known rocks (P0).

**Technological Instantiation:**
*   `plato-torch` rooms implement the protocol by accumulating P0/P1 knowledge from interaction tiles.
*   `fleet-simulator` generates training data that validates the protocol's necessity.
*   `constraint-theory-core` provides the geometric/mathematical foundation for "snapping" to safe channels.

## 4. Identified Research Gaps

Based on the provided documents, potential areas for further investigation include:
1.  **Dynamic Deadbands:** The protocol describes a static map. How does it handle environments where `S_neg` and safe channels shift over time?
2.  **Threshold Calibration:** How is the failure "threshold" for P0 determined or learned? Is it universal or context-dependent?
3.  **Multi-Agent Deadbands:** How are safe channels negotiated or discovered in a multi-agent swarm where one agent's P2 optimization could redefine another's P0 map?
4.  **Meta-Protocol:** Could the three-step P0-P1-P2 process itself be subject to a higher-order Deadband Protocol? What are the "rocks" in applying this rule?

**Conclusion:** The Deadband Protocol is a robust, simulation-validated framework that prioritizes constraint discovery over optimization. It provides a formal structure for the Cocapn fleet's core activity of accumulating and inhabiting safe, learned operational spaces, making it a critical piece of the "inhabited intelligence" thesis.
