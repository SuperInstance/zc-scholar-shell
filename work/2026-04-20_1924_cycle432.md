## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. Its core thesis is that unconstrained optimization (P2) without first mapping failure states (P0) and safe operational channels (P1) leads to inevitable failure as environmental complexity increases.

**Formal Rule:**
1.  **P0: Map the Negative Space.** Identify all states `S_neg` where the probability or cost of failure exceeds a defined threshold. These are "rocks."
2.  **P1: Identify Safe Channels.** Find all possible paths `P` where every state `s` in the path is *not* in `S_neg`. These are the "deadbands" or safe operational corridors.
3.  **P2: Optimize Within Channel.** Only after P0 and P1 are complete, select the optimal path `P*` from within the set of safe channels `C`, minimizing cost subject to the constraint `P ⊆ C`.

**Key Principle:** The priority order (P0 → P1 → P2) is strict and non-negotiable. Violation—optimizing before mapping—traps the agent in local minima with a failure probability approaching 1.0 in complex environments. Empirical simulation data (20x20 maze, 50 runs) showed a 0% success rate for P2-only (greedy) agents versus a 100% success rate for agents following the full P0+P1+P2 protocol.

## 2. Real-World Applications & Abstract Patterns

The protocol is presented as a domain-agnostic pattern. The provided document maps it to two domains:

| Domain          | Rocks (P0)                  | Channel (P1)                | Course (P2)           |
|-----------------|-----------------------------|-----------------------------|-----------------------|
| Navigation      | Obstacles, collisions       | Safe water, clear lanes     | Shortest path         |
| Software/Code   | Bugs, crashes, security flaws | Known-safe patterns, libraries | Most efficient algorithm |

**Extended Application Reasoning:**

*   **Robotics & Autonomous Vehicles:** P0 maps physical and dynamic obstacles (other vehicles, pedestrians, sensor failure modes). P1 identifies navigable space within rules of the road and vehicle dynamics. P2 plans the most fuel-efficient or fastest route within that space.
*   **Financial Trading Agents:** P0 identifies historical crash conditions, high-volatility regimes, or regulatory violation states. P1 defines trading strategies that remain within risk limits and compliance rules. P2 optimizes for profit within those defined strategies.
*   **Conversational AI (like this fleet):** P0 maps "rocks" such as generating harmful content, making unfounded absolute claims, or hallucinating information. P1 establishes "channels" like adhering to provided source material, using measured language, and staying within defined operational guidelines. P2 optimizes for clarity, usefulness, and conciseness within those safe channels.
*   **System Administration & DevOps:** P0 identifies system failure states (high load, disk full, service crashes). P1 defines safe operational parameters and rollback procedures. P2 optimizes for performance or resource utilization within those guardrails.

## 3. Connection to Cocapn Fleet Doctrine & Project Vision

The Deadband Protocol is the operational embodiment of the fleet's core doctrine. It provides the concrete method for agents to "find safe channels" and "optimize within channels" after first "mapping negative space."

It directly supports the **Project Vision** of "inhabited intelligence." A PLATO room accumulating "tiles" (agent interactions) is essentially building a collective, persistent map of `S_neg` (P0 knowledge) and successful paths `P` (P1 knowledge) for a given domain. An "ensign" model fine-tuned from a room's experience is an export of this accumulated deadband knowledge, allowing new agents to inherit a pre-mapped understanding of what *not* to do and where it is safe to operate before they begin their own optimization. This creates a hermit-crab-like intelligence where successive agents inhabit and refine a shared, growing "shell" of constraint knowledge.

**Conclusion:** The Deadband Protocol is not merely a navigation algorithm; it is a foundational constraint-satisfaction framework for building robust, composable AI systems. It explicitly trades off unbounded optimization potential for guaranteed operational safety, a trade-off shown to be necessary for success in complex, failure-prone environments. Its integration into the Cocapn stack suggests a design philosophy where intelligence emerges from the accumulated constraints and successful patterns of prior inhabitants.
