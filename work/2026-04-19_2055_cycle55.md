## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents operating in complex environments. It is formalized as a rule to prevent agents from becoming trapped in local minima by enforcing a sequence of constraint identification before optimization.

**Core Steps (Strict Priority):**
- **P0: Map the Negative Space** – Identify all states (S_neg) where the probability or cost of failure exceeds a defined threshold. These are "rocks" or failure zones. The agent must not proceed without this map.
- **P1: Identify Safe Channels** – Find all possible paths (P) where every state (s) along the path is *not* in the negative space (s ∉ S_neg). These paths are "deadbands" or safe operational channels.
- **P2: Optimize Within Channel** – From the set of safe channels (C), select the optimal path (P*) that minimizes cost, subject to the constraint that the path remains entirely within a safe channel. Optimization is never unconstrained.

**Key Principle:** An agent that attempts to optimize (P2) before completing the mapping of negative space (P0) and safe channels (P1) will, in complex environments, approach a failure probability of 1.0. Simulation data from a 20x20 maze (50 runs) showed a 0% success rate for P2-only (greedy) agents versus a 100% success rate for agents following the full P0+P1+P2 sequence.

## 3. Connection to Cocapn Fleet Vision

The Deadband Protocol operationalizes a key aspect of the "Inhabited Intelligence" thesis outlined in `PROJECT-VISION.md`. If intelligence is accumulated through inhabitation (agents as hermit crabs, tiles as scratches), then a primary mechanism for that accumulation is learning *constraints*—the "scratches" that mark past failures (P0 knowledge).

*   **Ghost Tiles as P0 Maps:** The concept of "ghost tiles" – lessons from dead agents – directly represents the accumulation of negative space knowledge (P0). A PLATO room that remembers these tiles provides a pre-mapped set of "rocks" for new inhabitant agents.
*   **Ensuring Safe Inhabitation:** The protocol ensures that new agents (crabs) can safely inhabit and contribute to a shell (room) without repeating catastrophic failures. They first learn the room's accumulated constraints (P0/P1) before optimizing their own contributions (P2).
*   **From Protocol to Persistent Memory:** The Deadband Protocol is the *process* an individual agent uses. The PLATO room system is the *persistent memory* that stores the outcomes of that process across generations of agents, turning individual constraint maps into collective, inhabitable knowledge.
