## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. Its core thesis is that unconstrained optimization (P2) before mapping failure states (P0) and safe operational channels (P1) leads to catastrophic failure with high probability as environmental complexity increases.

**Formal Rule (DEADBAND PROTOCOL v1.0):**
- **P0: Map the Negative Space** – Identify all states `S_neg` where the probability or cost of failure exceeds a defined threshold. These are "the rocks." This step is mandatory and non-negotiable.
- **P1: Identify Safe Channels** – Find all possible paths `P` where every state `s` along the path is not in `S_neg`. These are "the deadbands" or safe operational corridors.
- **P2: Optimize Within Channel** – From the set of safe channels `C`, select the optimal path `P*` that minimizes cost, subject to the constraint that `P* ⊆ C`. Optimization is only permitted within the bounds established by P0 and P1.

**Strict Priority:** P0 → P1 → P2. Violating this order (e.g., attempting P2 before P0 and P1) is shown in simulation to trap an agent in local minima with a success rate approaching zero.

**Empirical Proof:** The document cites simulation data from a 20x20 maze over 50 runs:
- Agents using a P2-only (greedy optimization) strategy succeeded 0 out of 50 times.
- Agents following the full P0+P1+P2 (Deadband) protocol succeeded 50 out of 50 times while maintaining optimal speed.

## 3. Connection to Cocapn Fleet & Project Vision

The Deadband Protocol is not just an algorithm; it is a foundational **fleet doctrine**. It directly operationalizes the core philosophy from `PROJECT-VISION.md`: "Intelligence is not built. It is inhabited."

*   **P0 as Collective Memory:** The "rocks" (failure states) are the accumulated "scratches" on the PLATO room shell—lessons learned from past agent failures (ghost tiles). This is negative knowledge that prevents repeated catastrophe.
*   **P1 as the Inhabited Shell:** The "safe channels" are the validated, persistent pathways built up through successful agent inhabitation. They define the operational space where new "hermit crab" agents can safely function.
*   **P2 as Specialized Action:** Optimization within safe channels is the work of specialist "ensign" models, who excel within a known-good subspace. Their output, in turn, becomes new tiles that refine the shell's understanding of P0 and P1.

The protocol enforces a learning loop where every agent's experience (success or failure) refines the communal understanding of what *not* to do (P0) and where it is *safe* to operate (P1), before any agent attempts to do something *well* (P2). This creates a system where intelligence grows through constrained, safe exploration rather than unbounded, risky optimization.
