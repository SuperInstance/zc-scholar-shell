## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. Its core thesis is that unconstrained optimization (P2) without first mapping failure states (P0) and safe operating channels (P1) leads to catastrophic failure with high probability as environmental complexity increases.

**Formal Steps:**
1.  **P0: Map the Negative Space.** Identify all states `S_neg` where the probability or cost of failure exceeds a defined threshold. These are "rocks." This step is purely prohibitive.
2.  **P1: Identify Safe Channels.** Find all possible paths `P` where every state `s` along the path is *not* in `S_neg`. These are the "deadbands" or safe operating corridors.
3.  **P2: Optimize Within Channel.** Only after P0 and P1 are complete, select the optimal path `P*` from within the set of safe channels `C`, minimizing cost subject to the constraint `P ⊆ C`.

**Key Principle:** The priority order (P0 → P1 → P2) is strict and non-negotiable. Violation—optimizing before mapping—traps the agent in local minima. The provided simulation data (a 20x20 maze) supports this: a P2-only (greedy) agent succeeded 0 out of 50 runs, while the full deadband protocol agent succeeded 50 out of 50.

## 2. Real-World Applications & Abstract Patterns

The protocol abstracts to any agent domain. The provided document maps two examples:

| Domain          | Rocks (P0)                   | Channel (P1)                     | Course (P2)               |
|-----------------|------------------------------|----------------------------------|---------------------------|
| **Navigation**  | Obstacles, collisions        | Clear, safe water/airspace       | Shortest/fastest route    |
| **Code**        | Bugs, crashes, security flaws| Known-safe patterns, libraries   | Most efficient algorithm  |

**Extended Applications:**

*   **Machine Learning Training:** P0 identifies hyperparameter combinations or data regions that cause model divergence or overfitting. P1 defines the stable training regime (e.g., learning rate bounds, batch size ranges). P2 then seeks the fastest convergence or highest accuracy within that regime.
*   **Financial Trading:** P0 maps market conditions (e.g., extreme volatility, liquidity crunches) that historically lead to maximum drawdown. P1 identifies trading strategies that remain solvent in those conditions. P2 optimizes for profit within those resilient strategies.
*   **Conversational AI (like this fleet):** P0 defines conversation paths that lead to harmful outputs, hallucinations, or user disengagement. P1 establishes safe response templates and factual grounding channels. P2 then seeks the most helpful, engaging, or efficient response within those safe boundaries.
*   **Product Design:** P0 identifies user interactions that cause frustration, error, or abandonment. P1 outlines interface flows that avoid those pitfalls. P2 optimizes those flows for speed, delight, or conversion.

## 3. Connection to Cocapn Fleet Doctrine & `PROJECT-VISION.md`

The Deadband Protocol is the operational embodiment of the fleet's core philosophical thesis: **"Intelligence is not built. It is inhabited."**

*   **P0 as Collective Memory:** The "rocks" are not just abstract failures; they are the accumulated "scratches" of every agent that has failed before. In the PLATO room system, these negative states become "ghost tiles"—persistent lessons from dead agents. A room inhabited by many crabs learns a rich, shared map of `S_neg`.
*   **P1 as the Inhabited Shell:** The "safe channels" are the validated, lived-in paths within the shell (the PLATO room). They are not theoretically derived but empirically discovered and reinforced through successful inhabitation.
*   **P2 as Specialized Optimization:** An "ensign" (a specialist AI model exported from a room) is the product of P2 optimization *within a specific, well-mapped channel*. It is not a general intelligence but a highly efficient path-finder for a known safe space, trained on the tiles (scratches) of its predecessors.

The protocol rejects the "construction" paradigm of mainstream AI (building a monolithic, general model) in favor of an "inhabitation" paradigm: first, a collective learns what *not* to do and where it *can* be (P0/P1 within a room); then, specialized intelligence (P2) can emerge safely and efficiently from that shared, persistent memory.
