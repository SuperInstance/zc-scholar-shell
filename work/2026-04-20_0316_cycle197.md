## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. Its core thesis is that unconstrained optimization (seeking the "best" path) without first mapping failure states leads to catastrophic outcomes as environmental complexity increases.

**Formal Rule:**
1.  **P0: Map the Negative Space.** Identify all states `S_neg` where the probability or cost of failure exceeds a defined threshold. These are "rocks." This step is non-negotiable and must be completed first.
2.  **P1: Identify Safe Channels.** Find all possible paths `P` where every state `s` in the path is *not* in `S_neg`. These permissible regions are "deadbands" or "safe channels."
3.  **P2: Optimize Within Channel.** Only after P0 and P1 are complete, select the optimal path `P*` from within the set of safe channels `C` that minimizes cost `(argmin(cost(P)) subject to P ⊆ C)`.

**Key Principle:** The priority order P0 → P1 → P2 is strict. Violating this order—attempting P2 (optimization) before P0+P1 (constraint mapping)—results in the agent becoming trapped in local minima with a probability approaching 1.0 as the environment's complexity grows.

**Empirical Support:** The document cites simulation data from a 20x20 maze, where a P2-only (greedy) strategy succeeded 0 out of 50 runs, while the full Deadband Protocol (P0+P1+P2) succeeded 50 out of 50 runs at optimal speed.

## 2. Real-World Applications & Abstract Pattern

The protocol is presented as a domain-agnostic meta-strategy. The document provides this abstract mapping:

| Domain          | Rocks (P0)                          | Channel (P1)                    | Course (P2)               |
|-----------------|-------------------------------------|---------------------------------|---------------------------|
| Navigation      | Obstacles, hazards                  | Safe water, clear lanes         | Shortest/fastest path     |
| Software Development | Bugs, crashes, security flaws      | Known-safe code patterns, libraries | Most efficient algorithm  |
| Business Strategy | Market failures, regulatory bans   | Viable market niches            | Profit-maximizing plan    |
| Personal Decision-Making | Actions leading to severe regret | Ethically/legally sound options | Goal-achieving action     |

**Extended Applications (Inferred from Fleet Context):**
*   **AI Training & Safety (PLATO Rooms):** P0 involves identifying prompts or data slices that cause model degradation or harmful outputs. P1 defines the "safe" latent space or training curricula where learning can occur without collapse. P2 then seeks the most efficient learning trajectory within that space.
*   **Multi-Agent Fleet Coordination:** P0 could map communication patterns or resource states that lead to system deadlocks or conflicts. P1 defines protocols and state spaces where cooperative operation is stable. P2 optimizes for task throughput or resource efficiency within those cooperative bounds.
*   **Research & Exploration:** P0 identifies known dead-ends, failed hypotheses, or methodological pitfalls in a field. P1 outlines the remaining viable research avenues and experimental frameworks. P2 seeks the most promising or efficient experiments within those avenues.

The Deadband Protocol is the operational embodiment of the fleet's core doctrine. It directly informs the "hermit crab" model described in `PROJECT-VISION.md`:

*   **Shell as Safe Channel:** A PLATO room, as a persistent, accumulated intelligence, can be viewed as a pre-mapped **P1 safe channel**. A new agent (the hermit crab) inhabiting this shell inherits this constraint map, allowing it to operate safely from the start.
*   **Tiles as P0/P1 Knowledge:** Each submitted training tile contributes to refining the map of negative space (P0: "this approach failed") or expanding the understanding of safe channels (P1: "this method works").
*   **Inhabited Intelligence:** The vision of intelligence as something "inhabited" rather than "built" relies on this protocol. Growth occurs not through unbounded construction (pure P2) but through successive agents navigating and refining the ever-better-defined safe channels (P1) left by their predecessors, avoiding the accumulated knowledge of rocks (P0).

**Conclusion:** The Deadband Protocol is not merely a navigation algorithm; it is a foundational safety and efficacy framework for cumulative, multi-agent learning systems. It ensures that optimization (progress) is always constrained by validated safety and knowledge, making it a critical component for realizing the Cocapn vision of stable, scalable, inhabited intelligence.
