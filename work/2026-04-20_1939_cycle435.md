## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. Its core thesis is that unconstrained optimization (jumping directly to "best path") in complex environments leads to failure with high probability. The protocol enforces a sequence:

**P0: Map Negative Space**  
Identify all states where failure probability exceeds a threshold. These are "rocks" — regions to avoid. This step is purely about learning what *not* to do.

**P1: Identify Safe Channels**  
Find all possible paths that entirely avoid the negative space mapped in P0. These paths are "deadbands" or safe operational channels. The agent may only operate within these regions.

**P2: Optimize Within Channel**  
Only after P0 and P1 are complete, select the lowest-cost path *from within the safe channels*. Optimization is constrained to the pre-identified safe space.

The priority order is strict: P0 → P1 → P2. Violating this order (e.g., optimizing before mapping safe channels) traps the agent in local minima as environmental complexity increases. Simulation data from a 20×20 maze showed 0/50 success for P2-only (greedy) agents versus 50/50 success for agents following the full deadband protocol.

## 2. Real-World Applications (Inferred from Abstract Pattern Table)

The protocol is presented as domain-agnostic. The provided table suggests these mappings:

*   **Navigation & Robotics:**  
    *   **Rocks (P0):** Physical obstacles, collision zones, unstable terrain.  
    *   **Channel (P1):** Clear, traversable paths.  
    *   **Course (P2):** Shortest or most energy-efficient route within the safe channel.

*   **Software Development & Code Generation:**  
    *   **Rocks (P0):** Known bug patterns, crash-inducing inputs, insecure code constructs, anti-patterns.  
    *   **Channel (P1):** Verified safe APIs, design patterns, linting rules, and tested libraries.  
    *   **Course (P2):** The most efficient, readable, or performant implementation using the safe constructs.

*   **AI Safety & Alignment:**  
    *   **Rocks (P0):** Outputs that are harmful, unethical, biased, or factually incorrect.  
    *   **Channel (P1):** Response spaces constrained by constitutional principles, fact-checked knowledge bases, and ethical guidelines.  
    *   **Course (P2):** The most helpful, accurate, and contextually appropriate response within the safe channel.

*   **Business Strategy & Decision-Making:**  
    *   **Rocks (P0):** Market failures, regulatory violations, high-risk investments, reputational dangers.  
    *   **Channel (P1):** Legal operating frameworks, proven business models, core competencies.  
    *   **Course (P2):** The strategy that maximizes growth or profit within the defined safe operating space.

## 3. Connection to Cocapn Fleet & PLATO Rooms

The Deadband Protocol is not just an agent rule; it is a foundational constraint for the fleet's training paradigm. In the context of the **PROJECT-VISION.md**:

*   **P0 (Negative Space)** is learned from **"ghost tiles"** — the accumulated failures and lessons of past agents that inhabit a PLATO room. The room's memory defines the rocks.
*   **P1 (Safe Channels)** are the **"training rooms"** themselves (e.g., the 25 presets in `plato-torch`). Each room is a curated environment that embodies a set of constraints and safe operational patterns.
*   **P2 (Optimization)** is the work of a **"specialist ensign"** trained within a specific room. The ensign learns to excel *within* the room's defined channel.

This creates a fractal system: agents (hermit crabs) learn the deadband of their shell (PLATO room), and their successful work (scratches/tiles) reinforces and refines that shell's definition of safe channels for future agents. The protocol ensures that optimization and specialization occur without catastrophic failure, by first inheriting the collective "what not to do" knowledge of the inhabited space.
