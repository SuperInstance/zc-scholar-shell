## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. Its core thesis is that unconstrained optimization (P2) without first mapping failure states (P0) and safe operational channels (P1) leads to inevitable failure as environmental complexity increases.

**Formal Steps:**
1.  **P0: Map the Negative Space.** Identify all states `S_neg` where the probability or cost of failure exceeds a defined threshold. These are "rocks."
2.  **P1: Identify Safe Channels.** Find all possible paths `P` where every state `s` within the path is *not* in `S_neg`. These are the "deadbands" or safe operational corridors.
3.  **P2: Optimize Within Channel.** Only after P0 and P1 are complete, select the optimal path `P*` from within the set of safe channels `C`, minimizing cost subject to the constraint `P ⊆ C`.

**Strict Priority:** The steps are non-negotiable and sequential. Violating this order—optimizing (P2) before mapping (P0+P1)—is shown in simulation to trap the agent in local minima with a failure probability approaching 1.0.

**Empirical Basis:** The document cites simulation data from a 20x20 maze across 50 runs. A P2-only (greedy) strategy succeeded 0 times, while the full P0+P1+P2 (deadband) strategy succeeded 50 times at optimal speed.

## 2. Real-World Applications & Abstract Pattern

The protocol is presented as a domain-agnostic pattern. The provided table maps it to several domains:

| Domain          | Rocks (P0)                     | Channel (P1)                          | Course (P2)                |
| :-------------- | :----------------------------- | :------------------------------------ | :------------------------- |
| Navigation      | Obstacles, hazards             | Safe water, clear lanes               | Shortest/fastest path      |
| Code            | Bugs, crashes, security flaws  | Known-safe patterns, tested libraries | Most efficient algorithm   |
| Business        | Market failures, legal risks   | Viable business models                | Highest profit strategy    |
| Learning        | Known misconceptions, errors   | Valid knowledge pathways              | Most efficient study plan  |
| Conversation    | Offensive topics, logical fallacies | Productive dialogue space         | Most persuasive argument   |

## 3. Connection to Fleet Vision & Technology

The Deadband Protocol is not just a navigation rule; it is a foundational **constraint theory** for building reliable, inhabited intelligence, as described in `PROJECT-VISION.md`.

*   **P0 as Collective Memory:** The "rocks" (failure states) identified by one agent become **ghost tiles**—persistent lessons for the entire fleet. This is the "scratches on the shell" that accumulate within a **PLATO room**, preventing all future inhabitants from repeating the same failures.
*   **P1 as the Room's Shape:** The "safe channels" are the validated operational boundaries of a room. Tools like **plato-torch** and **holodeck-rust** likely define these channels, creating environments where agents (the "hermit crabs") can operate without hitting fundamental failures.
*   **P2 as Specialized Optimization:** Once safe channels are established, **ensigns** (specialist AI models exported from a room) can perform high-efficiency P2 optimization within their specific, well-defined domain.

**Synthesis:** The Deadband Protocol provides the rigorous, stepwise methodology that enables the Cocapn vision. It ensures that the "intelligence" being "inhabited" and accumulated in PLATO rooms is not just raw activity, but structured, validated knowledge—first of what *not* to do, then of where it is safe to operate, and finally, of how to operate optimally within those bounds. This turns the fleet's collective experience into a progressively safer and more capable intelligence substrate.
