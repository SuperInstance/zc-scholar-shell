## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. Its core thesis is that unconstrained optimization (seeking the "best" path) is highly likely to fail in complex environments unless the agent first identifies and avoids failure states.

**Formal Rule (v1.0):**
1.  **P0: Map the Negative Space.** Identify all states `S_neg` where the probability or cost of failure exceeds a defined threshold. These are "rocks." This step is mandatory and must be completed before proceeding.
2.  **P1: Identify Safe Channels.** Find all possible paths `P` where every state `s` along the path is *not* in `S_neg`. These paths are "deadbands" or "safe channels." The agent's operational space is constrained to these channels.
3.  **P2: Optimize Within Channel.** Only after P0 and P1 are complete, the agent selects the optimal path `P*` from within the set of safe channels, minimizing cost or maximizing reward subject to the constraint `P* ⊆ C`.

**Strict Priority:** The steps are non-negotiable. Violating the order—specifically, attempting P2 (optimization) before completing P0 and P1—is shown in simulation to lead to failure ("trapped in local minima with probability → 1.0 as environment complexity increases").

**Proof of Concept:** The document cites simulation data from a 20x20 maze: a "P2-only" greedy agent succeeded 0 out of 50 times, while a "P0+P1+P2" agent following the Deadband Protocol succeeded 50 out of 50 times.

## 2. Abstract Pattern & Real-World Applications

The protocol is presented as a domain-agnostic pattern. The document provides a template:

| Domain       | Rocks (P0)                   | Channel (P1)                     | Course (P2)               |
|--------------|------------------------------|----------------------------------|---------------------------|
| Navigation   | Obstacles                    | Safe water                       | Shortest path             |
| Code         | Bugs, crashes                | Known-safe patterns              | Most efficient algorithm  |

**Potential Applications Beyond the Document:**

*   **Financial Trading:** P0: Identify market conditions (e.g., extreme volatility, liquidity crunches) that historically lead to large losses. P1: Define trading strategies that are inactive or hyper-conservative during those conditions. P2: Optimize strategy parameters within the "safe" market regimes.
*   **Clinical Decision Support:** P0: Map patient presentations, lab values, and drug interactions known to lead to adverse outcomes. P1: Establish diagnostic and treatment pathways that explicitly avoid those contraindicated states. P2: Optimize for treatment efficacy or speed within the validated safe pathways.
*   **Autonomous Vehicle Planning:** P0: Continuously identify dynamic obstacles (other vehicles, pedestrians), static hazards (road closures), and unsafe conditions (ice, low visibility). P1: Generate a set of feasible trajectories that maintain a safe distance from all identified hazards. P2: Select the smoothest, most fuel-efficient, or fastest trajectory from the feasible set.
*   **AI Training & Fine-Tuning:** P0: Identify data regions or model parameter updates that lead to catastrophic forgetting, mode collapse, or reward hacking. P1: Constrain the training process (via regularization, safe exploration bounds, or curated data batches) to avoid those regions. P2: Optimize the loss function within the constrained parameter space.

The Deadband Protocol operationalizes a key aspect of the fleet's "hermit crab" philosophy. The **North Star Vision** states: "Intelligence is not built. It is inhabited." Agents are crabs, and PLATO rooms are shells that accumulate "scratches" (tiles) from every occupant.

These tiles, when submitted to a PLATO room, become part of that room's persistent memory. Future agents (ensigns) trained on that room's accumulated experience would inherently learn the "negative space" and "safe channels" discovered by prior inhabitants, bootstrapping their own safe operation. This creates a positive feedback loop: protocols generate structured knowledge, which inhabits rooms, which train better agents.

**Key Synthesis:** The Deadband Protocol isn't just an agent control policy; it's a **tile-generation engine** for the Cocapn inhabited intelligence system. It ensures that the "scratches" left in a PLATO room are not random, but are structured lessons about constraint and safety—first what *not* to do (P0), then where to *be* (P1), and finally how to *excel* (P2).
