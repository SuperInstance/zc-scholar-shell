## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. Its core thesis is that unconstrained optimization (P2) before mapping failure states (P0) and safe operational channels (P1) leads to catastrophic failure with high probability as environmental complexity increases.

**Formal Rule (v1.0):**
- **P0: Map the Negative Space.** Identify all states `S_neg` where the probability or cost of failure exceeds a defined threshold. These are "the rocks."
- **P1: Identify Safe Channels.** Find all possible paths `P` where every state `s` in the path is not in `S_neg`. These are the "deadbands" or safe operational corridors.
- **P2: Optimize Within Channel.** Select the optimal path `P*` from within the set of safe channels `C`, minimizing cost subject to the constraint `P ⊆ C`.

**Strict Priority:** The steps are non-negotiable and sequential. An agent must not begin P2 without completing P0 and P1. Simulation data (20x20 maze, 50 runs) cited in the document shows a 0% success rate for P2-only (greedy) agents versus a 100% success rate for agents following the full P0+P1+P2 sequence.

**Extended Application Hypotheses:**

*   **Machine Learning Training:** P0 could involve identifying hyperparameter regions or data batches that cause model collapse or gradient explosion. P1 would define stable training regimes (e.g., learning rate ranges, batch compositions). P2 would then seek the fastest convergence within those stable regimes.
*   **Cybersecurity (Agent Action):** P0 would map network actions that trigger intrusion detection systems or cause system instability. P1 would identify stealthy, permissible action sequences. P2 would choose the most efficient sequence to achieve an objective without detection.
*   **Conversational AI:** P0 could identify user prompts or model responses that lead to safety violations, incoherence, or user disengagement. P1 would define response templates and reasoning pathways that avoid those states. P2 would optimize for helpfulness and engagement within those safe boundaries.
*   **Financial Trading:** P0 would identify market conditions or order types with high risk of catastrophic loss. P1 would define trading strategies that are robust across known market regimes. P2 would optimize for profit within the confines of those robust strategies.

## 3. Connection to Cocapn Fleet Doctrine & Project Vision

The Deadband Protocol is the operational embodiment of the fleet's core doctrine: "P0: Map negative space (what NOT to do), P1: Find safe channels (where you CAN be), P2: Optimize within channels (best path)."

This aligns directly with the **"Inhabited Intelligence"** thesis outlined in `PROJECT-VISION.md`. The protocol provides a concrete method for how a "hermit crab" agent should behave when inhabiting a "shell" (a PLATO room). The "scratches" (tiles) accumulated in a room would, in part, be records of collective P0 knowledge—shared mappings of negative space that all subsequent inhabitants can use, preventing them from having to relearn failures individually. The room itself becomes a persistent map of safe channels (P1) built from the accumulated experience of its inhabitants.

## 4. Identified Research Gaps / Questions

Based on the provided documents, several open questions arise:

1.  **Threshold Definition:** How is the failure threshold in P0 (`|failure(S_neg)| > threshold`) quantitatively or qualitatively defined? Is it static or adaptive?
2.  **Channel Discovery Complexity:** In high-dimensional, continuous state spaces (like natural language or pixel-based environments), how is the exhaustive identification of safe channels (P1) computationally feasible? The document implies this is critical but does not address scalability.
3.  **Dynamic Environments:** The protocol is described for a given state `S` and environment `E`. How does it handle non-stationary environments where the negative space (rocks) and safe channels drift over time?
4.  **Integration with Learning:** Could the outcomes of P2 (optimization attempts) feed back to refine the P0 map or discover new P1 channels? The document presents a strict, one-way sequence, but a learning system might benefit from a loop.
5.  **Empirical Validation Beyond Mazes:** The provided proof is a 20x20 maze simulation. Broader validation in the domains suggested (code, navigation, etc.) would strengthen the claim of universal applicability.

**Conclusion:** The Deadband Protocol presents a compelling, principled alternative to greedy optimization for AI agents. Its strength lies in its enforced discipline to seek safety first. Its integration into the Cocapn fleet's architecture suggests it is a foundational constraint for generating reliable, reusable training tiles within PLATO rooms, directly supporting the vision of intelligence as a collectively inhabited and accumulated state.
