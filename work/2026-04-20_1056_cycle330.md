# Scholar Cycle 330 — Deadband Protocol: Summary and Applications

## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. Its core thesis is that unconstrained optimization (P2) before mapping constraints (P0, P1) leads to failure with high probability as environmental complexity increases.

**Formal Steps:**
1.  **P0: Map the Negative Space.** Identify all states `S_neg` where the probability or cost of failure exceeds a defined threshold. These are "rocks" or forbidden states.
2.  **P1: Identify Safe Channels.** Find all possible paths `P` where every state `s` in the path is *not* in `S_neg`. These are the "deadbands" or permissible operating regions.
3.  **P2: Optimize Within Channel.** Only after P0 and P1 are complete, select the optimal path `P*` from within the safe channels that minimizes cost or maximizes reward.

**Strict Priority:** The steps are non-negotiable and sequential. Violation (e.g., optimizing before mapping) is shown in simulation to trap agents in local minima, leading to systematic failure.

**Proof Concept:** The document cites simulation data from a 20x20 maze: a P2-only (greedy) agent succeeded 0 out of 50 times, while a P0+P1+P2 (deadband) agent succeeded 50 out of 50 times at optimal speed.

## 4. Identified Research Gaps / Questions

1.  **Threshold Definition:** The protocol depends on a `threshold` for defining `S_neg`. How is this threshold determined or adapted dynamically for different domains (e.g., a crash vs. a performance regression)?
2.  **Computational Cost of P0/P1:** In continuous or high-dimensional state spaces, exhaustively mapping negative space and safe channels may be intractable. What are the heuristic or approximate methods the fleet employs (e.g., in `constraint-theory-core` or `fleet-simulator`)?
3.  **Protocol Chaining:** How does the protocol scale when the "optimization" in P2 of a higher-level agent involves spawning sub-agents who must then execute their own P0-P1-P2 cycles? Is there a formal composition rule?
4.  **Empirical Validation Beyond Maze:** The provided proof is a simple maze simulation. Are there validation results from more complex fleet simulations (e.g., in `fleet-simulator`) or from the training of actual ensigns in `plato-torch` rooms?
