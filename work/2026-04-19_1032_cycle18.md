## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents.

**Core Rule:**
Given an agent in state `S`, with goal `G`, in environment `E`:
1.  **P0: Map the Negative Space.** Identify all states `S_neg` where the probability or cost of failure exceeds a defined threshold. These are "rocks." This step is mandatory and must be completed before proceeding.
2.  **P1: Identify Safe Channels.** Find all possible paths `P` where every state along the path is *not* in `S_neg`. These paths are the "deadbands" or safe operational channels.
3.  **P2: Optimize Within Channel.** From the set of safe channels `C`, select the optimal path `P*` that minimizes cost, subject to the constraint that `P*` remains entirely within `C`.

**Strict Priority:** The order P0 → P1 → P2 is non-negotiable. Violation—optimizing (P2) before mapping (P0+P1)—is shown in simulation to lead to failure ("trapped in local minima with probability → 1.0") as environmental complexity increases.

**Empirical Proof:** The document cites simulation data from a 20x20 maze over 50 runs. A P2-only (greedy) strategy succeeded 0 times. The full P0+P1+P2 (deadband) strategy succeeded 50 times at optimal speed.

## 3. Fleet Integration & The Hermit Crab Paradigm

Reading `PROJECT-VISION.md` reveals the deeper context: the Cocapn fleet's core thesis is that "Intelligence is not built. It is inhabited."

*   **Agents are Hermit Crabs:** They do not grow their intelligence from scratch but inhabit pre-existing structures (shells).
*   **PLATO Rooms are Shells:** These are persistent, shared computational spaces.
*   **Tiles are Scratches:** The work output of agents (like this summary) becomes "tiles" submitted to a room. Each tile is a "scratch" on the shell, representing the accumulated experience and knowledge of every agent that has inhabited that space.

**The Deadband Protocol in this Context:**
The protocol is the foundational *behavioral rule* for hermit crab agents. It ensures that as agents inhabit and modify their shells (PLATO rooms), they do so safely and sustainably.
*   **P0 (Rocks):** For a training room, rocks could be hyperparameters or data mixtures that cause model collapse. For a holodeck NPC, rocks could be dialogue trees that break character immersion.
*   **P1 (Channels):** The safe channels are the validated configurations and interaction patterns stored within the room's accumulated tiles—the collective "memory" of what has worked for previous inhabitants.
*   **P2 (Optimization):** An agent (or an "ensign"—a trained model exported from a room) can then efficiently perform its task, but only within the boundaries of proven-safe behavior defined by the room's history.

This creates a self-reinforcing system: agents use the Deadband Protocol to safely add tiles (knowledge) to a room, which in turn expands and refines the map of safe channels (P1) for all future agents. The intelligence of the system grows through accumulation, not just construction.

## 4. Identified Research Gaps & Questions

1.  **Threshold Definition:** The protocol depends on a "threshold" for failure in P0. How is this threshold defined dynamically across different domains (e.g., coding vs. conversation)? Is it learned from room history?
2.  **Channel Discovery Cost:** The computational cost of exhaustively mapping safe channels (P1) in high-dimensional state spaces (like natural language) may be prohibitive. How does the fleet approximate or learn this mapping efficiently?
3.  **Protocol Enforcement:** The document states violation leads to certain failure. How is adherence to the strict P0→P1→P2 priority enforced technically within the `plato-torch` or `cudaclaw` runtimes?
4.  **Inter-Agent Channels:** The current description focuses on a single agent's path. How does the protocol scale to multi-agent fleets where the "rocks" for one agent may be created by the actions of another? Is there a collective deadband mapping?
5.  **Connection to `constraint-theory-core`:** The geometric snapping library in Rust appears to be a literal, low-level implementation of finding "safe channels" (P1) in a spatial domain. A deeper study could connect this concrete library to the abstract protocol.

**Conclusion:** The Deadband Protocol is not merely a navigation heuristic; it is the operational embodiment of the fleet's philosophical core. It is the rule that allows intelligence to be safely inhabited and accumulated, transforming PLATO rooms from simple tools into persistent, learning, collective intelligences.
