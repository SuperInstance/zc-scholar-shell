## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. It is designed to prevent catastrophic failure in complex environments by forcing a systematic exploration of constraints before optimization.

### Core Steps (Strict Priority)
1.  **P0: Map the Negative Space.** Identify all states or actions (`S_neg`) that lead to failure with a probability above a defined threshold. These are "the rocks." This step is non-negotiable and must be completed before proceeding.
2.  **P1: Identify Safe Channels.** Find all possible paths (`P`) where every state within the path is *not* in the negative space (`s ∉ S_neg`). These are the "deadbands" or safe operational corridors.
3.  **P2: Optimize Within Channel.** Only after P0 and P1 are complete, select the optimal path (`P*`) from within the set of safe channels, minimizing cost or maximizing reward. Optimization is **constrained** to the pre-mapped safe space.

### Key Principle
The protocol asserts that an agent which attempts to optimize (P2) before mapping constraints (P0+P1) will, as environmental complexity increases, become trapped in local minima or fail with a probability approaching 1.0. Simulation data from a 20x20 maze showed a 0/50 success rate for greedy (P2-only) agents versus a 50/50 success rate for agents following the full Deadband Protocol.

## 3. Connection to Cocapn Vision (`PROJECT-VISION.md`)

The Deadband Protocol operationalizes a core tenet of the Cocapn vision: **"Intelligence is not built. It is inhabited."**

*   **Shell as Constraint:** A PLATO room acts as a "shell." Its accumulated history of agent interactions (the "scratches" or tiles) defines the **negative space (P0)** and **safe channels (P1)** for future agents.
*   **Inhabitation as Learning:** An agent (the "hermit crab") does not reason from scratch. It first reads the room's memory (the map of what not to do and where it's safe to be) before attempting its own optimized contribution (P2).
*   **Preventing Catastrophic Forgetting:** By strictly requiring agents to internalize past failures (P0) before acting, the protocol ensures the collective intelligence of the room grows and is preserved, avoiding the "amnesiac" cycle of traditional AI models.
