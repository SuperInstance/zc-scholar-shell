## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. Its core thesis is that an agent must first identify failure states ("rocks") before it can safely navigate toward a goal. The protocol is formalized as:

**Step 1 — Map the Negative Space (P0)**
*   Identify all states `S_neg` where the probability or cost of failure exceeds a defined threshold.
*   This step defines what **not** to do. Proceeding without this map is a protocol violation.

**Step 2 — Identify Safe Channels (P1)**
*   Find all possible paths `P` where every state `s` in the path is **not** in the negative space (`s ∉ S_neg`).
*   These paths are the "deadbands" or safe operational channels.

**Step 3 — Optimize Within Channel (P2)**
*   From the set of safe channels `C`, select the optimal path `P*` that minimizes cost, subject to the constraint `P* ⊆ C`.
*   Optimization is **only** permitted within the bounds established by P0 and P1.

**Strict Priority:** P0 > P1 > P2. The simulation data provided (a 20x20 maze, 50 runs) indicates that agents using a P2-only (greedy optimization) approach had a 0% success rate, while agents following the full P0+P1+P2 protocol had a 100% success rate.

## 2. Abstract Pattern & Real-World Applications

The protocol is presented as a domain-agnostic pattern. Here are potential applications beyond navigation:

| Domain | Rocks (P0 - Negative Space) | Channel (P1 - Safe Space) | Course (P2 - Optimization) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Software Development** | Code patterns known to cause bugs, crashes, or security vulnerabilities (e.g., buffer overflows, SQL injection patterns). | Known-safe libraries, design patterns, and code review guidelines. | Selecting the most efficient, readable, or performant implementation from among the safe options. |
| **Financial Trading Agent** | Market conditions (volatility, liquidity crunches) or asset combinations historically linked to catastrophic loss. | Defined trading strategies with proven risk boundaries and stop-loss mechanisms. | Optimizing entry/exit points or portfolio weightings within the confines of the safe strategy. |
| **Content Moderation System** | Types of speech, imagery, or behavior that violate platform policy (e.g., hate speech, graphic violence). | Clear community guidelines and vetted communication channels. | Optimizing the speed or accuracy of classification for content that falls within the safe guidelines. |
| **Clinical Decision Support** | Treatment plans with known severe side effects for a given patient profile, or dangerous drug interactions. | Evidence-based treatment protocols and pharmacopeia guidelines. | Personalizing treatment timing or supportive care within the established clinical protocol. |
| **Conversational AI (This Agent)** | Outputs that are hallucinated, factually incorrect, violate safety filters, or disregard user instructions. | Responses grounded in provided source material (e.g., `fleet-knowledge/` files) and adhering to specified formats. | Making the summary more concise, better structured, or more clearly connected to the user's intent. |

## 3. Connection to Cocapn Fleet Doctrine

The Deadband Protocol is the operational embodiment of the fleet's core doctrine: **"P0: Map negative space (what NOT to do), P1: Find safe channels (where you CAN be), P2: Optimize within channels (best path)."**

This protocol directly informs the design of training systems like **plato-torch**, where a "DeadbandRoom" preset would enforce this decision hierarchy. An agent's failures within a room become "ghost tiles"—persistent `S_neg` states that train future agents to avoid the same pitfalls (P0 knowledge). The work of a **Zeroclaw** agent, by reading real files and producing factual output, is an exercise in staying within the "safe channel" of verified information.

**Conclusion:** The Deadband Protocol is a constraint-first optimization framework. It argues that robust intelligence in complex environments is not born from unconstrained goal-seeking, but from a disciplined process of first learning the boundaries of failure.
