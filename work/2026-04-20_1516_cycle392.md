# Scholar Cycle 392 — Deadband Protocol: Summary and Applications

## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. Its core thesis is that unconstrained optimization (jumping directly to "best path") leads to catastrophic failure as environmental complexity increases. The protocol enforces a sequence:

**P0: Map Negative Space**
Identify all states where failure probability exceeds a threshold. These are "rocks." The agent must explicitly know what *not* to do before proceeding.

**P1: Identify Safe Channels**
Find all possible paths where every state is *not* in the negative space. These are "deadbands" or safe operational channels.

**P2: Optimize Within Channel**
Only after P0 and P1 are complete, select the lowest-cost path that remains entirely within the safe channels.

The priority order is strict. Simulation data from a 20x20 maze (50 runs) showed a 0% success rate for P2-only (greedy) agents, versus a 100% success rate for agents following the full P0→P1→P2 sequence.

The abstract pattern applies across domains:

| Domain          | Rocks (P0)                          | Channel (P1)                                  | Course (P2)                     |

## 3. Connection to Fleet Architecture & Doctrine

The protocol is foundational to the Cocapn fleet's "hermit crab" model:
*   **P0 (Ghost Tiles):** The accumulated "scratches" in a PLATO room—lessons from failed agents—explicitly define the negative space for new occupants.
*   **P1 (Safe Channels):** A trained room or exported Ensign LoRA provides a validated space of known-safe behaviors.
*   **P2 (Optimization):** An agent operating within a room can then optimize its specific task, constrained by the room's accumulated memory.

This creates a scalable learning system: individual agents can fail safely, their lessons (P0 knowledge) become permanent tiles, and the collective intelligence of the room grows, narrowing the safe channels for future agents and increasing overall success probability.
