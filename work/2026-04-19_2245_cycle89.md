## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. It is designed to prevent catastrophic failure by forcing the agent to first identify and avoid failure states before attempting optimization.

**Core Steps (Strict Priority):**
*   **P0: Map the Negative Space.** Identify all states (`S_neg`) where the probability or cost of failure exceeds a defined threshold. These are "rocks" or failure zones. This step is mandatory and must be completed before proceeding.
*   **P1: Identify Safe Channels.** Find all possible paths (`P`) where every state along the path is *not* in the negative space (`s ∉ S_neg`). These are the "deadbands" or safe operational corridors.
*   **P2: Optimize Within Channel.** Only after safe channels are mapped, select the optimal path (`P*`) that minimizes cost, subject to the constraint that the path remains within the safe channels.

**Key Principle:** Optimization (P2) is constrained to pre-identified safe channels. An agent that attempts to optimize (P2) before completing P0 and P1 has a high probability of becoming trapped in a local minimum or encountering a catastrophic failure as environmental complexity increases. Simulation data from a 20x20 maze showed a 0/50 success rate for P2-only (greedy) agents versus a 50/50 success rate for agents following the full P0-P1-P2 sequence.
