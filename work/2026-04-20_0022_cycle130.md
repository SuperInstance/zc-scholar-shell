## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. It is designed to prevent catastrophic failure in complex environments by enforcing a sequence of constraint identification before optimization.

**Core Rule (Formal):**
Given an agent in state `S` with goal `G` in environment `E`:
1.  **P0: Map the Negative Space.** Identify all states `S_neg` where the probability or cost of failure exceeds a defined threshold. These are "rocks" or failure states. This step is mandatory and must be completed before proceeding.
2.  **P1: Identify Safe Channels.** Find all possible paths `P` where every state `s` along the path is *not* in `S_neg`. These paths are "deadbands" or safe operational channels.
3.  **P2: Optimize Within Channel.** From the set of safe channels `C`, select the optimal path `P*` that minimizes cost, subject to the constraint `P* ⊆ C`. Optimization is only permitted within the bounds established by P0 and P1.

**Strict Priority:** P0 > P1 > P2. The protocol states that an agent which optimizes (P2) before completing mapping (P0+P1) will, with high probability, become trapped in local minima or fail as environmental complexity increases.

**Empirical Basis:** The protocol cites simulation data from a 20x20 maze with 50 runs, where a P2-only (greedy) approach succeeded 0 times, while the full P0+P1+P2 (deadband) approach succeeded 50 times at optimal speed.

## 3. Connection to Cocapn's Core Thesis

The Deadband Protocol operationalizes a key aspect of the "Inhabited Intelligence" thesis outlined in `PROJECT-VISION.md`. If intelligence is accumulated through inhabitation (agents as hermit crabs, rooms as shells, tiles as scratches), then a primary mechanism for that accumulation is **learning what not to do**.

*   **P0 (Negative Space)** is the collective memory of failures—the "scratches" from hitting rocks. This knowledge is preserved in the room (e.g., as ghost tiles from dead agents) and becomes constitutive knowledge for future inhabitants.
*   **P1 (Safe Channels)** defines the viable operating space for an agent within a given inhabited shell. It is the accumulated wisdom of prior occupants.
*   **P2 (Optimization)** is the agent's novel contribution within the safe bounds of the shell.

Thus, the protocol ensures that individual agent optimization (P2) is always contextualized and constrained by the accumulated, shared experience of the inhabited space (P0+P1). It prevents agents from endlessly rediscovering failure modes and forces them to build upon, rather than ignore, communal knowledge.
