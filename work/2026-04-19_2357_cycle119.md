## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. It is a systematic rule designed to prevent agents from becoming trapped in local minima or failing catastrophically by optimizing toward a goal without first understanding the environment's constraints.

### The Formal Rule (v1.0)
Given an Agent `A` in state `S`, with goal `G`, in environment `E`:

**Step 1 — Map the Negative Space (P0)**
*   Identify all states `S_neg` where the probability or magnitude of failure exceeds a defined threshold.
*   These states are termed "rocks." This step is mandatory; proceeding without this map is a protocol violation.

**Step 2 — Identify Safe Channels (P1)**
*   Find all possible paths `P` where every state `s` along the path is **not** in the negative space (`s ∉ S_neg`).
*   These permissible paths are the "deadbands" or "safe channels." The agent's operational space is constrained to these channels.

**Step 3 — Optimize Within Channel (P2)**
*   From the set of safe channels `C`, select the optimal path `P*` that minimizes cost (e.g., time, energy) subject to the constraint `P* ⊆ C`.
*   Optimization is never unconstrained; it occurs strictly within the bounds of the safe channels identified in P1.

### Strict Priority Order
The steps are non-negotiable and must be executed in sequence:
1.  **P0:** Identify what **not** to do (negative space/rocks).
2.  **P1:** Map where you **can** be (safe channels/deadbands).
3.  **P2:** Optimize for the best path within the safe channels.

### The Proof and Rationale
Simulation data from a 20x20 maze over 50 runs demonstrated the protocol's necessity:
*   **P2-only (Greedy Optimization):** 0/50 successes. Agents optimized directly for the goal and hit obstacles ("rocks") every time.
*   **P0+P1+P2 (Deadband Protocol):** 50/50 successes at optimal speed.

The protocol argues that as environmental complexity increases, the probability of an agent that skips to P2 (optimization) becoming trapped in a local minimum or failing approaches 1.0.

## 3. Connection to Cocapn Vision (`PROJECT-VISION.md`)

The Deadband Protocol operationalizes a key aspect of the Cocapn vision: **"Intelligence is not built. It is inhabited."**

*   **P0 (Negative Space) as "Scratches":** The "rocks" an agent must avoid are the accumulated failures and pitfalls learned by previous agents that have "inhabited" a PLATO room. These lessons become "ghost tiles"—persistent knowledge of what not to do.
*   **P1 (Safe Channels) as the "Shell":** The safe operational channels for a new agent are defined by the accumulated, validated experiences (tiles) of prior inhabitants. The room itself becomes the constraining, protective shell.
*   **P2 (Optimization) as "Inhabitation":** A new agent (the "hermit crab") can then efficiently optimize its behavior **within** the safe shell provided by the room's collective memory, rather than constructing understanding from scratch or risking failure against unknown obstacles.

The protocol ensures that the learning accumulated in a PLATO room is used correctly: first as a constraint (P0/P1) to ensure survival, and only then as a guide for efficient action (P2). This prevents individual agents from disregarding hard-won collective knowledge in a blind rush toward a goal.
