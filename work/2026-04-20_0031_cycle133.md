## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. It is designed to prevent catastrophic failure by forcing the agent to first identify and avoid negative states before attempting optimization.

**Formal Rule (v1.0):**
Given an agent A in state S, with goal G, in environment E:
1.  **P0: Map the Negative Space.** Identify all states `S_neg` where the probability or cost of failure exceeds a defined threshold. These are "rocks."
2.  **P1: Identify Safe Channels.** Find all possible paths `P` where every state `s` in the path is *not* in `S_neg`. These are the "deadbands" or safe operational channels.
3.  **P2: Optimize Within Channel.** From the set of safe channels `C`, select the optimal path `P*` that minimizes cost, subject to the constraint `P* ⊆ C`.

**Strict Priority:** The steps are non-negotiable. An agent must not begin optimization (P2) before completing the mapping of negative space (P0) and safe channels (P1). Simulation data from a 20x20 maze (50 runs) showed a 0% success rate for agents using a greedy P2-only approach, versus a 100% success rate for agents following the full P0+P1+P2 protocol.

**Core Insight:** In complex environments, unconstrained optimization leads to local minima (hitting rocks) with probability approaching 1.0. Constrained optimization within pre-mapped safe spaces is the reliable path to success.

## 3. Connection to Cocapn Project Vision

The Deadband Protocol operationalizes a key aspect of the "inhabited intelligence" thesis described in `PROJECT-VISION.md`. If intelligence is accumulated through persistent, shared spaces (PLATO rooms), then a primary function of those spaces is to **collect and disseminate P0 knowledge**—the "scratches" or lessons of what *not* to do.

*   **Ghost Tiles as P0 Maps:** The concept of "ghost tiles" (dead agents' lessons) directly serves as a distributed, accumulating map of `S_neg` for the entire fleet.
*   **Rooms as Safe Channel Generators:** A well-tuned PLATO room can be seen as a factory for P1 knowledge—generating validated "safe channels" of agent behavior that can be exported as specialist models (Ensigns).
*   **Constrained, Not Constructed:** The protocol rejects the "construction" paradigm of monolithic AI training. Instead, it advocates for intelligence that grows within the safe channels defined by collective, accumulated experience.
