## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. Its core thesis is that unconstrained optimization (P2) before mapping failure states (P0) and safe operating channels (P1) leads to catastrophic failure as environmental complexity increases.

**Formal Rule:**
1.  **P0: Map the Negative Space.** Identify all states `S_neg` where the probability or cost of failure exceeds a defined threshold. These are "the rocks."
2.  **P1: Identify Safe Channels.** Find all possible paths `P` where every state `s` in the path is *not* in `S_neg`. These are the "deadbands" or safe operating corridors.
3.  **P2: Optimize Within Channel.** Only after P0 and P1 are complete, select the optimal path `P*` from within the safe channels that minimizes cost.

**Strict Priority:** P0 > P1 > P2. The protocol states that an agent which optimizes (P2) before completing mapping (P0+P1) will be trapped in local minima with a probability approaching 1.0 as complexity grows.

**Empirical Basis:** Simulation data from a 20x20 maze over 50 runs showed a 0% success rate for a P2-only (greedy) agent, versus a 100% success rate for the P0+P1+P2 (deadband) agent.

## 2. Real-World Applications & Abstract Pattern

The protocol is presented as a domain-agnostic pattern. The provided table suggests these mappings:

| Domain          | Rocks (P0)                          | Channel (P1)                    | Course (P2)               |
| :-------------- | :---------------------------------- | :------------------------------ | :------------------------ |
| Navigation      | Obstacles, collisions               | Clear paths, safe water         | Shortest/fastest route    |
| Software Development | Bugs, crashes, security flaws | Known-safe code patterns, libraries | Most efficient algorithm  |
| Business Strategy | Market failures, legal risks    | Validated business models       | Profit maximization       |
| Personal Decision-Making | High-cost mistakes (financial, health) | Choices with known acceptable outcomes | Preference optimization  |

## 3. Connection to Cocapn Fleet Vision (`PROJECT-VISION.md`)

The Deadband Protocol operationalizes a key aspect of the fleet's "inhabited intelligence" thesis. If intelligence is accumulated through persistent interaction (scratches on a shell), then a primary mechanism for that accumulation is **learning what not to do**.

*   **P0 as Collective Memory:** The "rocks" (`S_neg`) identified by one agent become negative knowledge tiles for the entire PLATO room. This is the accumulation of scratches that warn future inhabitants.
*   **P1 as Inherited Shell:** The "safe channels" are the validated corridors of operation that a new hermit crab (agent) inherits when it enters an inhabited shell (room). It doesn't start from zero.
*   **P2 as Local Adaptation:** Optimization within the safe channel is where an agent can express its individual specialty or adapt to novel sub-tasks, but always within the constraints of collective, accumulated safety knowledge.

Thus, the Deadband Protocol is not just a decision rule; it is a **learning and knowledge-transfer protocol** that ensures the swarm's intelligence grows safer and more robust over time by systematically encoding and respecting negative space.

## 4. Identified Research Gaps / Questions

1.  **Threshold Definition:** The protocol depends on a `threshold` for failure in P0. How is this threshold determined or adapted dynamically across different domains and rooms?
2.  **Channel Discovery Cost:** In continuous or high-dimensional state spaces, exhaustively finding *all* safe channels (P1) may be intractable. What are the heuristic or approximate methods for channel discovery that still satisfy the protocol's safety intent?
3.  **Inter-Agent Channel Conflict:** If multiple agents are operating in the same environment, one agent's "safe channel" (P1) might be invalidated by another agent's actions. How does the protocol scale to multi-agent systems?
4.  **Empirical Generalization:** The provided proof is a discrete maze navigation task. Further validation is needed in continuous control, natural language, and strategic reasoning domains to assess the protocol's general efficacy.
