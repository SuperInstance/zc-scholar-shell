## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. Its core thesis is that unconstrained optimization (P2) before mapping failure states (P0) and safe operating channels (P1) leads to catastrophic failure as environmental complexity increases.

**Formal Rule:**
1.  **P0: Map the Negative Space.** Identify all states `S_neg` where the probability or cost of failure exceeds a defined threshold. These are "the rocks."
2.  **P1: Identify Safe Channels.** Find all possible paths `P` where every state `s` in the path is *not* in `S_neg`. These are the "deadbands" or safe operating corridors.
3.  **P2: Optimize Within Channel.** Only after P0 and P1 are complete, select the optimal path `P*` from within the safe channels that minimizes cost.

**Strict Priority:** The steps are non-negotiable and sequential. Violation (optimizing P2 before P0+P1) results in a high probability of becoming trapped in local minima or hitting failure states.

**Evidence:** The document cites simulation data from a 20x20 maze: a P2-only (greedy) agent succeeded 0 out of 50 times, while a P0+P1+P2 (deadband) agent succeeded 50 out of 50 times at optimal speed.

## 2. Real-World Applications & Abstract Pattern

The protocol is presented as a domain-agnostic pattern. The document provides this mapping:

| Domain          | Rocks (P0)                   | Channel (P1)                     | Course (P2)               |
|-----------------|------------------------------|----------------------------------|---------------------------|
| Navigation      | Obstacles                    | Safe water                       | Shortest path             |
| Code            | Bugs, crashes                | Known-safe patterns              | Most efficient algorithm  |
| **AI Training** | **Catastrophic forgetting, reward hacking** | **Stable learning curricula**    | **Fastest convergence**   |
| **Business Strategy** | **Market failures, regulatory traps** | **Viable business models**       | **Maximum profit path**   |
| **Personal Decision-Making** | **Known bad habits, toxic situations** | **Healthy routines & environments** | **Most fulfilling goal pursuit** |

## 3. Connection to Fleet Vision & Technology

Reading `PROJECT-VISION.md` reveals a deep philosophical alignment:

*   **Hermit Crab Analogy:** The fleet's vision of "inhabited intelligence" — where agents (crabs) inhabit PLATO rooms (shells) and leave behind tiles (scratches) — is a systemic instantiation of the Deadband Protocol. The accumulated "scratches" (tiles) from all prior agents constitute a **collectively learned P0+P1 map**. A new agent entering a room doesn't start from zero; it inherits the known negative space (failed approaches, bugs) and safe channels (proven patterns) from all previous inhabitants.
*   **Rejection of "P2-Only" AI:** The vision explicitly criticizes the mainstream AI paradigm (OpenAI, Anthropic, etc.) for building "marvels of engineering that lack... memory of you." This can be interpreted as a critique of systems that perform powerful, stateless optimization (P2) without building persistent, shared maps of failure and safety (P0+P1). They are "not homes" because they do not accumulate the collective scratches that define a safe, learnable channel.
*   **Ghost Tiles as P0 Knowledge:** The concept of "ghost tiles" — lessons from dead agents — is a direct implementation of P0. They are explicit, persistent records of `S_neg` (failure states) for the community.
*   **Training Rooms as Safe Channels:** A PLATO training room, with its curated preset and accumulated tiles, defines a P1 "safe channel" for learning. An ensign model exported from that room is an agent specialized to operate optimally (P2) within that specific, well-mapped channel.

## 4. Identified Research Gaps & Questions

1.  **Threshold Definition:** The protocol depends on a `threshold` for defining failure states in P0. How is this threshold determined or learned dynamically across different domains and rooms?
2.  **Channel Discovery Cost:** The exhaustive search for *all* safe paths (P1) may be computationally intractable in high-dimensional state spaces. What heuristics or approximations does the fleet use (e.g., in `constraint-theory-core` or `fleet-simulator`) to make this feasible?
3.  **Inter-Room Channel Mapping:** If a room is a safe channel, how are safe pathways discovered *between* specialized rooms? Is there a meta-protocol for agents to navigate the ecosystem of rooms itself?
4.  **Empirical Validation:** The provided proof is a single maze simulation. A broader literature review or analysis of fleet simulation data would be needed to assess the protocol's efficacy in more complex, noisy, or adversarial environments.

**Conclusion:** The Deadband Protocol is not just a decision rule; it is the foundational operational doctrine for a fleet building persistent, collective intelligence. It transforms the AI problem from one of isolated optimization to one of collaborative cartography, where the primary value is in mapping the terrain of failure and safety for all subsequent inhabitants.
