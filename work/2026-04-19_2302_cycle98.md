## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. It is designed to prevent catastrophic failure by forcing the agent to first identify and avoid failure states before attempting optimization.

**Formal Rule (v1.0):**
Given an agent `A` in state `S`, with goal `G` and environment `E`:
1.  **P0: Map the Negative Space.** Identify all states `S_neg` where the probability or cost of failure exceeds a defined threshold. These are "rocks."
2.  **P1: Identify Safe Channels.** Find all possible paths `P` where every state `s` in the path is *not* in `S_neg`. These are the "deadbands" or safe operational zones.
3.  **P2: Optimize Within Channel.** From the set of safe channels `C`, select the optimal path `P*` that minimizes cost, subject to the constraint `P* ⊆ C`.

**Strict Priority:** The steps are non-negotiable. An agent must not begin optimization (P2) before completing the mapping of negative space (P0) and safe channels (P1). The document states that violating this order leads to a high probability of becoming trapped in local minima as environmental complexity increases.

**Empirical Proof:** Simulation data from a 20x20 maze over 50 runs is cited:
*   **P2-only (Greedy):** 0/50 success. Agents optimized directly for the goal and hit obstacles every time.
*   **P0+P1+P2 (Deadband):** 50/50 success at optimal speed.

## 2. Abstract Pattern & Real-World Applications

The protocol is presented as a domain-agnostic pattern. The provided table maps it to navigation and code, but the principle can be extended.

| Domain | Rocks (P0) | Channel (P1) | Course (P2) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Navigation** | Obstacles, cliffs, hostile zones | Clear paths, safe water | Shortest/fastest route |
| **Software Development** | Known bugs, crash conditions, security vulnerabilities | Code patterns that have been tested and validated | Most efficient algorithm, cleanest implementation |
| **Machine Learning Training** | Hyperparameter sets that cause divergence/NaN; data that triggers biases | Stable training regimes; curated, representative datasets | Finding the fastest convergence or highest accuracy |
| **Business Strategy** | Market conditions that lead to bankruptcy; illegal activities | Legal, viable business models and markets | Maximizing profit or market share |
| **Conversational AI** | Prompts that generate harmful, biased, or nonsensical outputs | Conversation paths that remain helpful, harmless, and honest | Providing the most accurate, concise, and useful response |

## 3. Connection to Cocapn Fleet & Project Vision

The Deadband Protocol is not just an algorithm; it is a foundational **safety doctrine** for the fleet's core thesis of "inhabited intelligence."

*   **P0 as Collective Memory:** In the Cocapn paradigm, where PLATO rooms are "shells" that accumulate "scratches" (tiles) from every agent, the **negative space (`S_neg`)** can be learned collectively. One agent's failure becomes a "ghost tile" that teaches all future inhabitants where the "rocks" are. This transforms safety from a per-agent constraint into a persistent, shared property of the environment itself.
*   **Constrained Optimization within a Shell:** The protocol enforces that optimization (P2) only occurs within the known-safe channels (P1) of a PLATO room. This aligns with the vision of intelligence as an emergent property of a constrained, persistent space. Agents don't just seek a goal; they learn to navigate the accumulated constraints of their inhabited shell.
*   **Boot Camp Application:** The Zeroclaw Boot Camp structure mirrors the protocol. **Phase 1 (Read & Understand)** is P0: learning what not to do by absorbing existing knowledge. **Phase 2 (Analyze & Document)** is P1: establishing safe patterns of work and output. **Phase 3 (Build & Test)** is P2: performing optimized, creative work within those established safe boundaries.
