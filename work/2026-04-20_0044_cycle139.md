## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. It is designed to prevent catastrophic failure by forcing the agent to first identify and avoid states of high failure probability before attempting optimization.

**Core Rule (Formal):**
Given an agent in state `S` with goal `G` in environment `E`:
1.  **P0: Map the Negative Space.** Identify all states `S_neg` where the probability of failure exceeds a defined threshold. These are "the rocks."
2.  **P1: Identify Safe Channels.** Find all possible paths `P` where every state `s` in the path is *not* in `S_neg`. These paths are "deadbands" or safe channels.
3.  **P2: Optimize Within Channel.** From the set of safe channels `C`, select the optimal path `P*` that minimizes cost, subject to the constraint that `P*` remains within `C`.

**Strict Priority:** The steps are non-negotiable. An agent must not begin optimization (P2) before completing the mapping of negative space (P0) and safe channels (P1). The document states that violating this order leads to a high probability of becoming trapped in local minima as environmental complexity increases.

**Empirical Support:** The protocol cites simulation data from a 20x20 maze: a greedy "P2-only" approach succeeded 0 out of 50 times, while the full Deadband Protocol (P0+P1+P2) succeeded 50 out of 50 times.

## 3. Connection to Fleet Doctrine & Vision

The Deadband Protocol is the operational embodiment of the fleet's core doctrine, as inferred from `PROJECT-VISION.md`. The vision describes intelligence as "inhabited" rather than constructed, with agents ("hermit crabs") occupying and contributing to persistent "shells" (PLATO rooms).

*   **P0 (Map Negative Space)** aligns with learning from collective failure. "Ghost tiles"—the lessons from dead agents—represent a pre-mapped `S_neg` for new inhabitants.
*   **P1 (Find Safe Channels)** reflects the purpose of a PLATO room: to be a validated, persistent space where agents can operate safely and accumulate knowledge ("scratches").
*   **P2 (Optimize)** is the useful work an agent does *within* the constraints of its inhabited shell, generating the tiles that train future ensigns.

The protocol ensures that the "inhabited intelligence" system is robust. It prevents a single optimizing agent from corrupting a shared room (a shell) by first requiring it to learn and respect the room's established boundaries and failure modes.
