## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. It is designed to prevent catastrophic failure by forcing the agent to first identify and avoid failure states before attempting optimization.

**Core Steps (Strict Priority):**
*   **P0: Map the Negative Space.** Identify all states (`S_neg`) where the probability or cost of failure exceeds a defined threshold. These are "rocks." This step is mandatory and must be completed before proceeding.
*   **P1: Identify Safe Channels.** Find all possible paths (`P`) where every state along the path is *not* in the negative space (`s ∉ S_neg`). These paths are "deadbands" or safe operational channels.
*   **P2: Optimize Within Channel.** Only after safe channels are mapped, select the optimal path (`P*`) from within them, minimizing cost subject to the constraint of staying within the safe channel.

**Key Principle:** Optimization (P2) performed without first completing P0 and P1 leads to a high probability of failure ("trapped in local minima") as environmental complexity increases. Simulation data cited in the document (a 20x20 maze) showed a 0/50 success rate for a "P2-only (greedy)" agent versus a 50/50 success rate for an agent following the full Deadband Protocol.

*   **Hermit Crab Analogy:** An agent (the crab) does not generate its own constraints from scratch. It inherits and operates within a "shell" of accumulated knowledge—the mapped negative space (P0) and safe channels (P1) that constitute a PLATO room's memory.

This makes the inhabited intelligence more robust over time.
