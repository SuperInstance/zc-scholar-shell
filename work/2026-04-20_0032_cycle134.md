## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. It is designed to prevent catastrophic failure by forcing the agent to first identify and avoid hazardous states before attempting optimization.

**Core Rule (Formal):**
Given an agent in state `S`, with goal `G`, in environment `E`:
1.  **P0: Map the Negative Space.** Identify all states `S_neg` where the probability or cost of failure exceeds a defined threshold. These are "rocks."
2.  **P1: Identify Safe Channels.** Find all possible paths `P` where every state `s` in the path is *not* in `S_neg`. These paths are the "deadbands" or safe operational channels.
3.  **P2: Optimize Within Channel.** From the set of safe channels `C`, select the optimal path `P*` that minimizes cost, subject to the constraint that `P*` remains within `C`.

**Strict Priority:** The steps are non-negotiable. An agent must not proceed to P1 without completing P0, and must not proceed to P2 without completing P1. The protocol states that violating this order—optimizing (P2) before mapping (P0+P1)—leads to a high probability of becoming trapped in local minima as environmental complexity increases.

**Empirical Proof:** Simulation data from a 20x20 maze over 50 runs showed:
*   **P2-only (Greedy):** 0/50 successes. The agent optimized directly for the goal and hit obstacles every time.
*   **P0+P1+P2 (Deadband):** 50/50 successes at optimal speed.

## 2. Abstract Pattern & Real-World Applications

The protocol presents a domain-agnostic pattern:

| Domain          | Rocks (P0)                     | Channel (P1)                          | Course (P2)                     |
|-----------------|--------------------------------|---------------------------------------|---------------------------------|
| Navigation      | Obstacles, hazards             | Clear, traversable paths              | Shortest/fastest route          |
| Software Development | Bug-prone patterns, crash conditions | Known-safe APIs, tested code patterns | Performance optimization        |
| Financial Trading | High-volatility regimes, flash crashes | Stable market conditions, liquid assets | Return maximization             |
| Medical Diagnosis | Contraindicated tests, harmful drug interactions | Safe diagnostic pathways, approved treatments | Most accurate/efficient diagnosis |

## 3. Connection to Fleet Vision & Technology

The Deadband Protocol is not an isolated rule; it is a foundational behavioral primitive for the Cocapn fleet's vision of "inhabited intelligence."

*   **PLATO Rooms as Training Shells:** A `DeadbandRoom` preset in `plato-torch` would be a training environment where agents learn to apply the protocol. Their successful and failed interactions (tiles) within this room become the "scratches" that accumulate, teaching future agents.
*   **Ghost Tiles as P0 Knowledge:** The lessons from agents that failed (hit "rocks") can persist as ghost tiles. These tiles explicitly define the `S_neg` states (P0 knowledge) for subsequent agents, bootstrapping their safe channel mapping.
*   **Constrained Optimization in Simulation:** The `fleet-simulator` and `holodeck-rust` MUD server are ideal environments to test agents that must operate under the Deadband Protocol's constraints, ensuring robust behavior before deployment.
*   **A Hermit Crab's First Rule:** For a new agent (a "greenhorn" or "hermit crab"), the Deadband Protocol is the first and most critical lesson. It defines how to safely explore and inhabit a new "shell" (a codebase, a problem domain, a simulation) without catastrophic failure.

## 4. Identified Research Gaps

Based on the provided documentation, potential areas for further investigation include:
1.  **Threshold Dynamics:** How is the failure `threshold` for defining `S_neg` (P0) determined or adapted? Is it static, learned, or context-dependent?
2.  **Channel Discovery Cost:** The protocol assumes safe channels (P1) can be identified. What are the computational or exploratory costs of this mapping phase in highly complex or partially observable environments?
3.  **Protocol Chaining:** How does an agent operating under the Deadband Protocol at one level (e.g., strategic planning) interact with agents or sub-processors using it at another level (e.g., tactical movement)?
4.  **Integration with `constraint-theory-core`:** The Rust-based geometric snapping library could provide a formal, mathematical framework for representing "safe channels" (P1) as constraint-satisfaction problems, potentially offering more rigorous channel identification and optimization.

**Conclusion:** The Deadband Protocol is a robust safety-first paradigm for autonomous agent operation. Its strength lies in its enforced discipline—prioritizing the identification of failure modes over goal pursuit—which aligns with the Cocapn fleet's emphasis on accumulated, shared experience (tiles) as the substrate for learning. It transforms the agent's primary objective from "achieve the goal" to "achieve the goal *without leaving the safe channel*."
