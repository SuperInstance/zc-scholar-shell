## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. Its core tenet is that optimization (P2) is only safe and effective when performed within a pre-mapped "safe channel" (P1), which itself is defined by first exhaustively identifying "negative space" or failure states (P0).

**Formal Rule:**
1.  **P0: Map the Negative Space.** Identify all states `S_neg` where the probability or cost of failure exceeds a defined threshold. These are "the rocks."
2.  **P1: Identify Safe Channels.** Find all possible paths `P` where every state `s` along the path is **not** in `S_neg`. These are the "deadbands" or safe operational channels.
3.  **P2: Optimize Within Channel.** From the set of safe channels `C`, select the optimal path `P*` that minimizes cost, subject to the constraint `P* ⊆ C`.

**Strict Priority:** The steps are non-negotiable and sequential. Violation—attempting P2 before completing P0 and P1—is shown in simulation to lead to failure ("trapped in local minima with probability → 1.0") as environmental complexity increases.

**Empirical Proof:** The document cites simulation data from a 20x20 maze: a "P2-only" greedy optimizer succeeded 0 out of 50 times, while the full P0+P1+P2 deadband protocol succeeded 50 out of 50 times.

## 2. Abstract Pattern & Real-World Applications

The protocol is presented as a domain-agnostic pattern. The provided table gives two examples:

| Domain       | Rocks (P0)                   | Channel (P1)                     | Course (P2)               |

| Navigation   | Obstacles (walls, cliffs)    | Safe water / clear paths         | Shortest path             |
| Code         | Bugs, crashes, security flaws| Known-safe patterns & libraries  | Most efficient algorithm  |

**Extended Applications (Inferred from Fleet Context):**

*   **AI Training & PLATO Rooms (P0):** Identify training data or interaction patterns that lead to model degradation, harmful outputs, or catastrophic forgetting. **(P1):** Define constrained training "rooms" or curricula that avoid those failure modes. **(P2):** Optimize learning rate or architectural choices within the safe curriculum.
*   **Multi-Agent Fleet Coordination (P0):** Map communication patterns or resource conflicts that cause system deadlocks or thrashing. **(P1):** Establish interaction protocols and resource allocation boundaries that prevent those conflicts. **(P2):** Optimize task scheduling or load balancing within the stable protocol.
*   **Research & Development (P0):** Actively document known failed approaches, dead ends, and invalid assumptions from past work (akin to "ghost tiles"). **(P1):** Delineate the remaining viable research directions based on the negative map. **(P2):** Pursue the most promising hypothesis within the confirmed viable space.

## 3. Connection to Cocapn Project Vision

The Deadband Protocol operationalizes a key implicit principle in the `PROJECT-VISION.md`: **intelligence through safe, accumulated experience.**

*   **Hermit Crab Analogy:** The "shell" (PLATO room) is a **P1 Safe Channel**. It is a persistent structure that has been validated by previous inhabitants to avoid "rocks" (failure states). An agent (the crab) doesn't build a shell from scratch while avoiding predators; it finds a proven-safe one and then optimizes its life within it **(P2)**.
*   **Tiles as Scratches:** Each submitted work "tile" is a data point that helps refine the map of both the negative space **(P0)** and the safe channels **(P1)** for future agents. A "ghost tile" from a failed agent is a direct contribution to P0 knowledge.
*   **Rejection of "Construction-Only" AI:** The vision criticizes major AI labs for focusing primarily on unconstrained optimization **(P2)**—building ever-larger models—without a robust, shared, and accumulating understanding of failure modes **(P0)** and safe operational envelopes **(P1)**. Cocapn's room-based system is designed to institutionalize the P0 and P1 steps.
