## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. Its core thesis is that unconstrained optimization (P2) before mapping constraints (P0, P1) leads to failure with high probability as environmental complexity increases.

**Formal Rule (v1.0):**
1.  **P0: Map the Negative Space.** Identify all states `S_neg` where the probability or cost of failure exceeds a defined threshold. These are "rocks." This step is mandatory and must be completed before proceeding.
2.  **P1: Identify Safe Channels.** Find all possible paths `P` where every state `s` along the path is *not* in `S_neg`. These paths are "deadbands" or safe operational channels. The agent is restricted to operating only within these channels.
3.  **P2: Optimize Within Channel.** From the set of safe channels `C`, select the optimal path `P*` that minimizes cost, subject to the constraint that `P*` remains entirely within `C`. Optimization is never unconstrained.

**Strict Priority:** P0 → P1 → P2. The protocol states that any agent optimizing (P2) before completing P0 and P1 will become trapped in local minima with probability approaching 1.0 in complex environments.

**Empirical Proof:** Simulation data from a 20x20 maze over 50 runs showed:
*   **P2-only (Greedy):** 0/50 successful completions. The agent optimized directly for the goal and hit obstacles every time.
*   **P0+P1+P2 (Deadband):** 50/50 successful completions at optimal speed.

## 2. Abstract Pattern & Real-World Applications

The protocol presents a domain-agnostic pattern:

| Domain          | Rocks (P0)                          | Channel (P1)                          | Course (P2)                          |
|-----------------|--------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| **Navigation**  | Obstacles, hazards                  | Clear lanes, safe water               | Shortest/fastest path within lanes  |
| **Software Development** | Bug-prone patterns, crash conditions | Known-safe APIs, tested code patterns | Most efficient implementation        |
| **Machine Learning** | Data leakage, overfitting regions   | Validated model architectures, hyperparameter ranges | Highest accuracy model within safe bounds |
| **Business Strategy** | Regulatory violations, market failures | Compliant, proven business models     | Most profitable strategy             |
| **Conversational AI** | Toxic outputs, factual hallucinations | Grounded, verifiable response patterns | Most helpful/engaging response       |

## 3. Connection to Cocapn Fleet Vision

The Deadband Protocol operationalizes a key principle from the `PROJECT-VISION.md`: intelligence as inhabitation and accumulation.

*   **P0 as Collective Memory:** The "rocks" (negative space) are not just abstract constraints; they are the accumulated "scratches" of past agent failures within a PLATO room. A room that has been inhabited learns where its previous occupants crashed, building a shared map of `S_neg`.
*   **Safe Channels as Emergent Intelligence:** The safe channels (P1) are the validated paths discovered by the swarm of "hermit crab" agents. They represent the room's growing, inhabited intelligence—not a single model's knowledge, but the collective operational memory of the space.
*   **Constrained Optimization as Sustainable Growth:** By forcing optimization (P2) to occur only within safe channels, the protocol ensures that agents contribute to the room's intelligence (by staying within known-good patterns) rather than destructively exploring. This aligns with the vision of intelligence that grows through persistent, shared accumulation.

**Conclusion:** The Deadband Protocol is more than a navigation rule; it is a foundational constraint for building inhabited intelligence. It ensures that the optimization drives of individual agents (crabs) are channeled into reinforcing and refining the collective knowledge of their shell (the PLATO room), creating a system where safe, intelligent behavior emerges from and is recorded by the environment itself.
