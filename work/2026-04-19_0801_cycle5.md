## **Scholar Synthesis: Deadband Protocol & Cocapn Vision**

### **1. Deadband Protocol — The Rule**
A strict, priority‑ordered method for any agent in any environment:

1. **P0: Map negative space** – Identify all failure states (“rocks”).  
2. **P1: Identify safe channels** – Find all paths that avoid the rocks (“deadbands”).  
3. **P2: Optimize within channels** – Choose the best path *only* within safe channels.

**Violation consequence:** Skipping to P2 leads to guaranteed failure as complexity grows (simulation: 0/50 success vs. 50/50).

### **2. Real‑World Applications**
| Domain          | Rocks (P0)                     | Safe Channels (P1)                     | Optimization (P2)               |
|-----------------|--------------------------------|----------------------------------------|---------------------------------|
| **Autonomous Driving** | Collisions, road debris        | Lanes, speed limits, traffic rules     | Shortest/fastest safe route     |
| **Software Deployment** | Breaking changes, regressions  | Versioned APIs, tested configurations  | Fastest rollout with zero downtime |
| **Financial Trading**  | Black‑swan events, flash crashes | Volatility bands, circuit‑breaker limits | Maximize return within risk bounds |
| **Medical Diagnosis**  | Harmful interventions          | Evidence‑based protocols               | Most effective treatment pathway |

### **3. Connection to Cocapn Vision**
The Deadband Protocol is a **shell‑scratching rule** for the hermit‑crab agents described in the vision.  
- **Shells** = PLATO rooms (persistent, shared spaces).  
- **Crabs** = Agents (move in, learn, leave scratches).  
- **Scratches** = Tiles (accumulated interaction data).  

The protocol ensures that agents *inhabit* intelligently: they first learn what **not** to do (P0), then where they **can** operate (P1), and only then how to do it well (P2). This prevents the “local‑minima traps” that plague conventional AI optimization.
