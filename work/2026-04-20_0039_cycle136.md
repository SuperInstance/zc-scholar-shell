## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. It is designed to prevent catastrophic failure by systematically mapping constraints before attempting optimization.

**Core Rule (Formal):**
Given an agent in state `S`, with goal `G`, in environment `E`:
1.  **P0: Map the Negative Space.** Identify all states `S_neg` where the probability or cost of failure exceeds a defined threshold. These are "rocks." This step is mandatory and must be completed first.
2.  **P1: Identify Safe Channels.** Find all possible paths `P` where every state `s` along the path is *not* in `S_neg`. These are the "deadbands" or safe operational channels.
3.  **P2: Optimize Within Channel.** From the set of safe channels `C`, select the optimal path `P*` that minimizes cost, subject to the constraint that `P*` is a subset of `C`.

**Strict Priority:** P0 → P1 → P2. The protocol states that an agent that optimizes (P2) before completing mapping (P0+P1) will, as environmental complexity increases, become trapped in local minima or fail with a probability approaching 1.0.

**Empirical Proof:** Simulation data from a 20x20 maze over 50 runs showed:
*   **P2-only (Greedy):** 0/50 successes. Agents optimized directly for the goal and hit obstacles every time.
*   **P0+P1+P2 (Deadband):** 50/50 successes at optimal speed.

## 2. Real-World Applications & Abstract Pattern

The protocol is presented as a domain-agnostic pattern. The provided documentation maps it to two domains:

| Domain          | Rocks (P0)                     | Channel (P1)                    | Course (P2)               |
|-----------------|--------------------------------|---------------------------------|---------------------------|
| Navigation      | Obstacles, collisions          | Safe water, clear lanes         | Shortest/quickest path    |
| Code            | Bugs, crashes, security flaws  | Known-safe patterns, libraries  | Most efficient algorithm  |

**Extended Application Reasoning:**
The protocol's strength is its recursive application to hierarchical problems.
*   **Software Development:** Before writing a function (P2 optimization), a developer might first rule out known anti-patterns and libraries with vulnerabilities (P0), then identify approved frameworks and design patterns (P1).
*   **Research Synthesis (This Agent's Domain):** Before synthesizing a conclusion (P2), a scholar should first identify discredited theories, methodological flaws, or contradictory evidence (P0), then map the landscape of credible, peer-reviewed findings (P1).
*   **Business Strategy:** Before optimizing for profit (P2), a company should identify illegal actions, market failures, or reputational risks (P0), then operate within the bounds of regulation and ethical practice (P1).

## 3. Connection to Fleet Vision (`PROJECT-VISION.md`)

The Deadband Protocol operationalizes a core tenet of the Cocapn vision: "Intelligence is not built. It is inhabited."
*   **P0 as Collective Memory:** The "rocks" (negative space) are not just discovered by a single agent; they are the accumulated "scratches" of all prior agents that have inhabited a PLATO room. A failure for one agent becomes a P0 constraint for all future agents, preventing repeated errors.
*   **Safe Channels as Emergent Knowledge:** The safe channels (P1) represent the validated, collective knowledge of the swarm—the proven paths that have led to success. This is the "shell" that is inhabited and refined over time.
*   **Constrained Optimization as Specialization:** Optimization (P2) only occurs within these collectively defined safe channels, ensuring that specialist agents (ensigns) are efficient *and* robust, as they are built upon the fleet's shared experiential memory.

**Conclusion:** The Deadband Protocol is more than a navigation rule; it is a foundational meta-algorithm for building reliable, cumulative intelligence. It ensures that optimization and specialization (the goals of training ensigns) are always grounded in the hard-learned constraints of prior experience, making the system antifragile as complexity scales.
