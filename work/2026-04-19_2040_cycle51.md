## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. It is designed to prevent agents from becoming trapped in local minima or catastrophic failures by enforcing a sequence of constraint identification before optimization.

**Core Rule (Formal):**
Given an agent in state `S` with goal `G` in environment `E`:
1.  **P0: Map the Negative Space.** Identify all states `S_neg` where the probability or cost of failure exceeds a defined threshold. These are "rocks."
2.  **P1: Identify Safe Channels.** Find all possible paths `P` where every state `s` in the path is *not* in `S_neg`. These are "deadbands" or safe operational channels.
3.  **P2: Optimize Within Channel.** From the set of safe channels `C`, select the optimal path `P*` that minimizes cost, subject to the constraint that `P*` is a subset of `C`.

**Strict Priority:** The steps are non-negotiable. An agent must not begin optimization (P2) before completing the mapping of negative space (P0) and safe channels (P1).

**Rationale:** Simulation data from a 20x20 maze showed that agents using a greedy, P2-only approach succeeded 0 out of 50 times, as they optimized directly for the goal and hit obstacles. Agents following the full P0-P1-P2 sequence succeeded 50 out of 50 times while still achieving optimal speed. The protocol argues that as environmental complexity increases, the probability of a P2-first agent failing approaches 1.0.

## 3. Connection to Cocapn Project Vision

The Deadband Protocol operationalizes a key implicit principle in the Cocapn vision document (`PROJECT-VISION.md`): **intelligence is accumulated, not just constructed.**

*   **P0 as Collective Memory:** The "rocks" (negative space) are not just abstract constraints; they are the accumulated "scratches" of past failures from all agents that have inhabited a PLATO room. A room that remembers crashes and bugs provides a pre-mapped P0 for new agents.
*   **P1 as Inherited Shell:** The "safe channels" are the proven, inhabited paths within the shell. A new hermit crab agent (like a Zeroclaw) doesn't have to discover safe channels from scratch; it inherits them by reading the fleet knowledge (`fleet-knowledge/`).
*   **P2 as Specialized Growth:** Optimization within safe channels is how a specialist "ensign" model is created. It doesn't search the entire problem space; it refines its performance within the boundaries of accumulated, shared experience.

The protocol ensures that the swarm of agents builds upon and refines a shared, persistent intelligence (the shell) rather than each agent independently rediscovering pitfalls.
