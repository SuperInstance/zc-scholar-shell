## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. It is designed to prevent catastrophic failure by forcing the agent to first identify and avoid failure states before attempting optimization.

**Formal Rule (v1.0):**
Given an agent in state `S`, with goal `G`, in environment `E`:
1.  **P0: Map the Negative Space.** Identify all states `S_neg` where the probability or cost of failure exceeds a defined threshold. These are "the rocks."
2.  **P1: Identify Safe Channels.** Find all possible paths `P` where every state `s` in the path is *not* in `S_neg`. These are "the deadbands" or safe operational zones.
3.  **P2: Optimize Within Channel.** From the set of safe channels `C`, select the optimal path `P*` that minimizes cost, subject to the constraint that `P*` is a subset of `C`.

**Strict Priority:** P0 > P1 > P2. The protocol states that any agent that attempts optimization (P2) before completing the mapping of negative space (P0) and safe channels (P1) will, as environmental complexity increases, become trapped in local minima with a probability approaching 1.0.

**Empirical Proof:** Simulation data from a 20x20 maze over 50 runs showed a 0% success rate for a P2-only (greedy) agent, while the full P0+P1+P2 (deadband) agent succeeded 100% of the time at optimal speed.

## 2. Abstract Pattern & Real-World Applications

The protocol presents a domain-agnostic pattern:

| Domain          | Rocks (P0)                          | Channel (P1)                    | Course (P2)                     |
|-----------------|-------------------------------------|---------------------------------|---------------------------------|
| Navigation      | Obstacles, hazards                  | Clear, traversable paths        | Shortest/fastest safe route     |
| Software Development | Bug-prone patterns, crash conditions | Known-safe APIs and idioms      | Most efficient implementation   |
| Financial Trading | High-volatility events, flash crashes | Stable, liquid market regimes   | Highest risk-adjusted return    |
| Medical Diagnosis | Contradictory symptoms, fatal conditions | Plausible diagnostic pathways   | Most likely correct diagnosis   |

## 3. Connection to Cocapn Fleet Vision (Based on `PROJECT-VISION.md`)

The Deadband Protocol operationalizes a core tenet of the Cocapn vision: **intelligence as inhabitation, not construction.**

*   **P0 as Collective Memory:** The "rocks" (`S_neg`) are not just abstract obstacles; they are the accumulated scratches in the PLATO room's shell—the recorded failures of every prior agent (the "ghost tiles"). Mapping negative space is an act of reading collective memory to avoid past mistakes.
*   **P1 as the Inhabited Shell:** The "safe channels" are the validated, persistent paths carved out by successful agents. They define the habitable space within the intelligence. An agent doesn't build this shell from scratch; it finds and moves into the pre-existing, community-validated structure.
*   **P2 as Individual Contribution:** Optimization within the safe channel is where the individual agent's unique contribution occurs. This successful traversal then becomes a new "scratch" (training tile) submitted to the PLATO room, refining the collective map for future agents.

The protocol ensures that the swarm intelligence of the fleet grows safely and cumulatively. Each agent's work (P2 optimization) is constrained by the hard-won knowledge of the community (P0+P1), and its successful output in turn reinforces and expands that communal knowledge base.

## 4. Identified Research Gaps & Fleet Context

1.  **Threshold Definition:** The protocol mentions a failure "threshold" for defining `S_neg` but does not specify how this threshold is set, learned, or adapted dynamically by the room or the agent. This is a key parameter for practical implementation.
2.  **Channel Discovery Complexity:** The method for exhaustively or probabilistically finding all safe channels (P1) in high-dimensional, complex state spaces (like code or strategic decision-making) is not detailed. The fleet's `constraint-theory-core` and `fleet-simulator` projects may be addressing this.
3.  **Integration with Training:** How does the Deadband Protocol, as a runtime decision rule, integrate with the training mechanisms of `plato-torch` and `plato-ensign`? Are P0 constraints baked into model weights via LoRA adapters, or are they enforced at inference time by the room server?
4.  **Empirical Scope:** The provided proof is from a discrete 2D maze simulation. Further validation is needed in the fleet's primary domains: code generation (`cudaclaw`), simulation (`holodeck-rust`), and geometric reasoning (`constraint-theory-core`).

**Conclusion:** The Deadband Protocol is not merely an agent navigation rule; it is the foundational safety and coordination mechanism for Cocapn's vision of inhabited, cumulative intelligence. It explicitly prevents the "local minima" of isolated, amnesiac AI systems by tethering individual agent optimization to the persistent, shared memory of the community.
