## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. It is designed to prevent catastrophic failure in complex environments by enforcing a sequence that maps constraints before optimization.

**Formal Rule (v1.0):**
Given an agent in state `S`, with goal `G`, in environment `E`:
1.  **P0: Map the Negative Space.** Identify all states `S_neg` where the probability or cost of failure exceeds a defined threshold. These are "rocks." This step is mandatory.
2.  **P1: Identify Safe Channels.** Find all possible paths `P` where every state `s` in the path is *not* in `S_neg`. These are the "deadbands" or safe operational corridors.
3.  **P2: Optimize Within Channel.** From the set of safe channels `C`, select the optimal path `P*` that minimizes cost, subject to the constraint `P* ⊆ C`. Optimization is only permitted within the bounds established by P0 and P1.

**Strict Priority:** P0 > P1 > P2. The protocol states that an agent which optimizes (P2) before completing constraint mapping (P0+P1) will, with high probability, become trapped in a local minimum or fail as environmental complexity increases.

**Empirical Basis:** Simulation data from a 20x20 maze over 50 runs showed a 0% success rate for a P2-only (greedy) agent, versus a 100% success rate for an agent following the full P0+P1+P2 sequence.

## 3. Connection to Cocapn Vision (`PROJECT-VISION.md`)

The Deadband Protocol operationalizes a core tenet of the Cocapn vision: **"Intelligence is not built. It is inhabited."**

*   **Shell as Constraint:** A PLATO room acts as the "shell" (the safe channel P1). Its accumulated "scratches" (tiles) from previous agents form a collective memory of what works and, critically, what does not (the negative space P0).
*   **Hermit Crab as Agent:** An agent (the "crab") does not invent its constraints from scratch. It moves into an existing shell (room) and its operation is inherently guided by the accumulated P0 knowledge (failures) of all prior inhabitants. Its optimization (P2) is bounded by this inherited wisdom.
*   **Anti-Fragility through P0:** The system's strength grows not just from success, but from the persistent, shared memory of failure ("ghost tiles"). This makes the fleet's collective intelligence robust against repeated mistakes.

**Conclusion:** The Deadband Protocol is the applied control theory for creating inhabited intelligence. It ensures that agents, like hermit crabs, leverage the accumulated constraints of their environment to operate safely and effectively, turning collective historical experience into a navigable map for future action.
