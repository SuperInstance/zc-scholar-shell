## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. Its core thesis is that unconstrained optimization (P2) before mapping constraints (P0) and safe spaces (P1) leads to failure with high probability as environmental complexity increases.

**Formal Rule (v1.0):**
Given an Agent A in state S, with goal G, in environment E:
1.  **P0: Map the Negative Space.** Identify all states `S_neg` where the probability or cost of failure exceeds a defined threshold. These are "the rocks."
2.  **P1: Identify Safe Channels.** Find all paths `P` where every state `s` in the path is *not* in `S_neg`. These are the "deadbands" or safe operational channels.
3.  **P2: Optimize Within Channel.** From the set of safe channels `C`, select the optimal path `P*` that minimizes cost, subject to the constraint that `P*` is a subset of `C`.

**Strict Priority:** P0 > P1 > P2. The protocol states that any agent optimizing (P2) before completing P0 and P1 will become trapped in local minima as complexity rises.

**Empirical Proof:** Simulation data from a 20x20 maze over 50 runs showed:
*   **P2-only (Greedy):** 0/50 successes. The agent optimized directly for the goal and hit obstacles every time.
*   **P0+P1+P2 (Deadband):** 50/50 successes at optimal speed.

## 3. Connection to Cocapn Vision (`PROJECT-VISION.md`)

The Deadband Protocol operationalizes a key aspect of the Cocapn vision: **"Intelligence is not built. It is inhabited."**

*   **Shell as Constraint:** A PLATO room acts as the "shell" or the **P1 safe channel**. It defines the bounded space (through its accumulated tiles and training presets) where agent learning can safely occur.
*   **Scratches as P0/P2 Data:** The "scratches" (tiles) submitted by agents contain both negative examples (P0: failed interactions, corrections) and optimized solutions (P2: successful patterns). The room accumulates this constraint and optimization knowledge.
*   **Hermit Crab Agents:** Agents ("crabs") do not build intelligence from scratch. They enter a pre-existing shell (a room with a history), operate within its safe channels (P1), contribute new scratches (P0/P2 data), and eventually export an adapted "ensign" model. This is a form of **inhabited learning**, where the environment (the room) encodes the critical P0 and P1 knowledge that individual agents can then safely optimize within.

The protocol provides the mechanistic rule that prevents agents from damaging their shell—by forcing them to identify the room's boundaries (P0) and safe operational modes (P1) before attempting to optimize their own behavior (P2). This ensures the shell's integrity and the cumulative value of its scratches over time.
