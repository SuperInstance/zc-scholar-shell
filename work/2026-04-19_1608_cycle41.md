## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. It is designed to prevent catastrophic failure by forcing the agent to first identify and avoid known failure states before attempting optimization.

**Core Steps (Strict Priority):**
*   **P0: Map the Negative Space.** Identify all states (`S_neg`) where the probability or cost of failure exceeds a defined threshold. These are "the rocks." This step is mandatory and must be completed before proceeding.
*   **P1: Identify Safe Channels.** Find all possible paths (`P`) where every state along the path is *not* in the negative space (`s ∉ S_neg`). These viable paths are "the deadbands" or safe operating channels.
*   **P2: Optimize Within Channel.** Only after safe channels are mapped does the agent select the optimal path (`P*`) from within them, minimizing cost subject to the constraint of staying within the safe channels.

**Key Principle:** Optimization (P2) is **constrained** to the safe channels identified in P1. An agent that attempts P2 before completing P0 and P1 is likely to become trapped in a local minimum or fail completely as environmental complexity increases. Simulation data from a 20x20 maze showed a 0% success rate for P2-only (greedy) agents versus a 100% success rate for agents following the full P0+P1+P2 protocol.

## 2. Real-World Applications (Abstracted from Fleet Context)

The protocol presents a generalizable pattern for structuring agent decision-making across domains.

| Domain | Rocks (P0 - What NOT to do) | Channel (P1 - Where you CAN be) | Course (P2 - Best path) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Code Generation & Review** | Code patterns that cause bugs, security vulnerabilities, or crashes. Known anti-patterns. | Syntactically correct, type-safe code that uses known-safe libraries and established patterns. | The most efficient, readable, and performant implementation within the safe patterns. |
| **Autonomous Navigation** | Physical obstacles, restricted zones, or hazardous terrain. | Clear, traversable paths verified by sensors. | The shortest or fastest route that remains on the safe paths. |
| **Conversational AI** | Responses that are toxic, factually incorrect, or violate safety guidelines. | Responses that are helpful, harmless, and truthful based on verified information. | The most engaging, informative, or concise response within the bounds of safety and truth. |
| **Financial Trading Agent** | Trades that violate compliance rules or carry extreme, unbounded risk. | Trading strategies that operate within defined risk parameters and regulatory limits. | The strategy with the highest expected risk-adjusted return within the defined safe parameters. |
| **Fleet Training (PLATO Rooms)** | Training data or interactions that lead to model degradation, instability, or harmful behaviors. | The space of "training tiles" and interaction sequences that have been validated as useful and safe by the room server. | The most efficient learning curriculum or tile sequence that stays within the validated space. |

## 3. Connection to Fleet Vision (`PROJECT-VISION.md`)

The Deadband Protocol operationalizes a key aspect of the fleet's "inhabited intelligence" thesis. It provides a concrete method for agents (the "hermit crabs") to safely navigate and learn within their "shells" (PLATO rooms).

*   **P0 (Negative Space):** This can be seen as the accumulated "scratches" of failure—lessons from dead agents preserved as "ghost tiles." A room's memory of what *not* to do defines its negative space.
*   **P1 (Safe Channels):** These are the proven, viable behaviors and knowledge pathways that have been validated by previous inhabitants of the room. They represent the room's cultivated wisdom.
*   **P2 (Optimization):** A new agent (a "greenhorn") can then safely optimize its performance *within* the room's established safe channels, accelerating learning without repeating catastrophic mistakes.

The protocol ensures that intelligence grows through safe, accumulated experience (inhabitation) rather than through unconstrained, and potentially dangerous, trial-and-error.

**Conclusion:** The Deadband Protocol is not just a navigation rule but a foundational safety and learning constraint for autonomous systems. It enforces a discipline of **constrained optimization**, where performance is pursued only within a well-mapped space of known-safe operations. This aligns directly with the Cocapn fleet's goal of building persistent, collective intelligence that remembers past failures to enable safer future exploration.
