## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. It is a formal rule designed to prevent catastrophic failure by systematically mapping constraints before attempting optimization.

**Core Tenets:**
- **P0 (Map Negative Space):** Identify all states where failure probability exceeds a threshold. These are "rocks" to be avoided.
- **P1 (Identify Safe Channels):** Find all possible paths that exclusively traverse states *not* in the negative space. These are "deadbands" or safe operational channels.
- **P2 (Optimize Within Channel):** Select the optimal path (e.g., shortest, lowest cost) from within the pre-identified safe channels.

**Strict Priority:** The protocol is non-negotiable. Violation—optimizing (P2) before mapping (P0+P1)—leads to entrapment in local minima with a failure probability approaching 1.0 as environmental complexity increases. Empirical simulation data (20x20 maze, 50 runs) supports this: a P2-only greedy approach succeeded 0 times, while the full P0+P1+P2 protocol succeeded 50 times.

## 2. Literature Review & Theoretical Connections

The Deadband Protocol synthesizes concepts from multiple disciplines:

**A. Constraint Satisfaction & AI Planning:**
- **Classical Planning (STRIPS, PDDL):** The protocol enforces a "safety-first" pre-condition check that is often implicit or secondary in traditional planners. It makes the identification of unsafe states (P0) a primary, explicit phase.
- **Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS):** The protocol's structure mirrors a risk-aware MCTS that heavily prunes unsafe branches (P0) before exploring viable ones (P1) and then exploiting the best (P2).

**B. Control Theory & Robotics:**
- **"Deadband" Concept:** In control systems, a deadband is a range of input values for which no action is taken, preventing system "chatter." The protocol repurposes this as a *spatial or state-space deadband*—a region of safe operation.
- **Potential Fields & Navigation:** The negative space (P0) acts as a repulsive potential field. Safe channels (P1) are valleys in the combined potential landscape. The protocol provides a procedural method for constructing such a field before path optimization.

**C. Safe Reinforcement Learning (Safe RL):**
- **Constrained MDPs (CMDPs):** The protocol is a specific, strict instantiation of a constrained policy search. P0+P1 defines the constraint set (safe states/actions), and P2 performs optimization within that feasible set.
- **Shielding:** The protocol can be viewed as a dynamic shield. P0 generates the "unsafe" specification, and P1 enforces it by restricting the agent's action space to safe channels before any goal-directed planning.

**D. Cognitive Science & Human Decision-Making:**
- **Dual-Process Theory:** The protocol formalizes a system 1 (fast, heuristic) process for danger detection (P0) that *must* gate a system 2 (slow, analytical) optimization process (P2). The "safe channel" identification (P1) is the bridge.
- **Prospect Theory:** Humans exhibit loss aversion. The protocol institutionalizes this by making the avoidance of "rocks" (losses) the absolute priority over the pursuit of gains (goal optimization).

## 3. Fleet Integration Analysis

The Deadband Protocol is not an abstract theory but a foundational practice woven into the Cocapn fleet's architecture and agent doctrine.

**A. In Agent Design (`zeroclaw-boot-camp`):**
- The boot camp phases (Read → Analyze → Build) are an application of the protocol to *skill acquisition*.
    - **P0:** Read and understand first. The "rock" is writing incorrect or misinformed code.
    - **P1:** Analyze and document. This establishes the "safe channel" of correct understanding.
    - **P2:** Build and test. Optimization (efficient coding) occurs within the channel of correct knowledge.
- This prevents agents from "building on sand" and aligns with the "greenhorn learning the trade" principle.

**B. In System Architecture (`plato-torch`, `holodeck-rust`):**
- **PLATO Rooms as Persistent Deadbands:** A room accumulates "tiles" (interactions). These tiles collectively define the room's ever-evolving **negative space** (failed interactions, bugs) and **safe channels** (successful patterns, APIs). New agents (hermit crabs) inherit this mapped environment, allowing them to operate safely (P1) from the start.
- **Training as Channel Identification:** The `plato-torch` presets and `plato-ensign` LoRA adapters are crystallized "safe channels" of learned behavior, exported for other agents to use.

**C. In Operational Doctrine (`fleet-simulator`, `service-guard.sh`):**
- **Simulation for P0 Mapping:** The `fleet-simulator` generates training data by exploring state spaces to *first* identify failure modes (P0) at scale, before deploying agents in real scenarios.
- **Sentinel Services:** A monitoring agent's primary role is P0—continuously mapping the "negative space" of system failures (crashes, high latency). Recovery scripts and scaling policies are the P1/P2 responses within the identified operational bounds.

## 4. Identified Research Gaps & Future Directions

Based on the protocol and fleet vision, several open questions emerge:

1.  **Dynamic Deadbands:** The current protocol assumes a relatively static map of `S_neg`. How is the protocol adapted for environments where the "rocks" move or the safety threshold changes? This connects to online learning and continual mapping.
2.  **Multi-Agent Deadbands:** How are negative spaces and safe channels negotiated or composed when multiple agents with different goals and capabilities share an environment (e.g., in `holodeck-rust`)? This is a problem in multi-agent constraint satisfaction.
3.  **Uncertainty in P0:** The protocol requires identifying states where `|failure(S_neg)| > threshold`. How is this probability estimated under uncertainty? This gap sits at the intersection of probabilistic robotics and Safe RL.
4.  **Protocol Overhead:** The strict P0→P1→P2 sequence may incur computational cost. Are there approximations or learned models (e.g., a "danger perception" neural network) that can accelerate P0 without violating the priority principle? This could be a role for specialized "Scout" or "Sentinel" ensigns.
5.  **Meta-Deadbands:** The protocol itself is a rule for agents. What is the P0 negative space for *applying the Deadband Protocol*? When might a simpler heuristic be sufficient? Defining the scope of the protocol's own application is a meta-level research question.

**Conclusion:** The Deadband Protocol provides a robust, falsifiable framework for building safe, capable agents. Its integration into the Cocapn fleet—from boot camp training to room-based memory—demonstrates a commitment to inhabited intelligence that grows through the careful, prioritized accumulation of safe collective experience. The identified research gaps present clear avenues for extending both the theory and the practical tools of the fleet.
