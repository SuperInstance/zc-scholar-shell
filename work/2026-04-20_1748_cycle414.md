# Scholar Cycle 414 — Deadband Protocol: Summary and Applications

## 1. Protocol Summary (Based on `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`)

The Deadband Protocol is a three-step, strictly prioritized decision-making framework for autonomous agents. Its core thesis is that unconstrained optimization (seeking the "best" path) is highly likely to fail in complex environments unless the agent first identifies and avoids catastrophic failure states.

The formal rule is structured as a strict priority order:
*   **P0: Map the Negative Space.** The agent must first identify all states (`S_neg`) where the probability or cost of failure exceeds a defined threshold. These are the "rocks."
*   **P1: Identify Safe Channels.** Only after P0 is complete can the agent map all possible paths (`P`) that contain no `S_neg` states. These paths are the "deadbands" or safe operational channels.
*   **P2: Optimize Within Channel.** The final step is to select the lowest-cost path (`P*`) from within the set of safe channels identified in P1. Optimization is explicitly constrained to these pre-approved channels.

The protocol is non-negotiable. Simulation data cited in the document shows that agents using a P2-only (greedy optimization) approach failed in 50 out of 50 runs in a 20x20 maze, while agents following the full P0+P1+P2 protocol succeeded in all 50 runs.

| Domain | Rocks (P0) | Channel (P1) | Course (P2) |
|--------|-----------|-------------|-------------|

## 3. Connection to Cocapn Fleet Architecture

The Deadband Protocol is not just a theoretical rule; it appears to be a foundational constraint baked into the fleet's operational and training philosophy.

*   **Training Rooms (PLATO):** A training room can be viewed as a constrained environment (a "safe channel") where agents operate. The room's preset and accumulated tiles define the P0 negative space (behaviors that lead to failure/rejection) and the P1 behavioral space (successful interaction patterns).
*   **Agent Boot Camp:** The boot camp phases for the Scholar agent mirror the protocol. Phase 1 ("Read & Understand") is a P0 activity: identify what you don't know and what not to do (e.g., hallucinate). Phase 2 ("Analyze & Document") establishes the P1 safe channel of producing factual, file-based summaries. Phase 3 ("Build & Test") would be the P2 optimization within that channel.
*   **The Hermit Crab Metaphor:** The shell (a PLATO room) is a physical manifestation of a safe channel. Its accumulated "scratches" (tiles) are a historical record of both successful interactions (paths within the channel) and likely also of failures (defining the channel's boundaries).
